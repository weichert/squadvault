Tests the platform/Writer's-Room separation in practice. |
| §9.2 artisan-frame fit | High. | **Highest.** | High. |

**The criteria do not converge.** Different candidates lead the table on different rows.

A tighter framing helps. The candidates partition along **three product axes**, and the Phase 11 first-surface question collapses to a choice among axes more than a choice among candidates:

- **Axis I — Historical-archival depth.** Surface the substrate's longitudinal data. Cluster A.
- **Axis II — Consumption-layer depth.** Polish the path from approved-artifact to league-member-reading-it. Cluster E.
- **Axis III — Content-class breadth.** Add a second artifact class beyond Weekly Recap. Cluster F (and, less directly, A1–A3 in their content-class-as-artifact reading).

The doctrine does not pick among the axes. The Reset Memo binds *which* surfaces are eligible; it does not bind *which axis advances first.* That is product judgment under §9.2's artisan-frame primary success criterion.

**Leading hypothesis: Axis II, candidate E1.** Reasoning:

- E1 leads on two of the five criteria (Sunday/Monday use; §9.2 artisan-frame fit). No other candidate leads on more than one.
- E1 is the lowest-implementation-risk candidate with a non-trivial product surface (A-cluster is also low-risk but lower learning value).
- The Operational Plan v1.1 closing-note framing (*"Build the button. Press it. Watch what happens. Then the rest of this plan tells you what to do next"*) reads as the founder's own framing of the next move, and that move is exactly Axis II / E1.

Phase B substantially overlaps with Axis II. A Phase 11 first-surface that is Axis II would not duplicate Phase B but would formalize it as a registered product surface.

**Why "leading hypothesis" rather than selection:** see §6.

## 6. The deferral — what is making the pick hard, and what would resolve it

The selection is deferred. Per session-brief Anti-Drift rule #1: deferral is a valid output. Per Anti-Drift rule #6: silence-over-speculation applies to the memo itself; where the analysis cannot resolve the pick from the text, defer.

**What is making the pick hard:**

1. **Axis II's leading position depends on a layering interpretation the Reset Memo does not adjudicate.** If "Phase 11 first surface" = "Operational Phase B's deliverable, formalized as a registered product surface," then E1 picks itself. If Phase 11 is a distinct framework above Operational Phase B (and Operational Phase B advances independently as operational work), then the Phase 11 first surface is a *product-vision-tier* choice that may legitimately advance content-class breadth (F1) or historical-archival depth (A) before consumption-layer polish. The session brief does not foreclose either reading. The Reset Memo, on the May 10 verification, does not either. **The surface-specification session must adjudicate this layering question explicitly** — and that adjudication is itself product judgment, not a doctrinal lookup.

2. **F1's admissibility is contingent on substrate-readiness against its v1 contract.** Without a probe, Axis III's leading candidate has an unknown implementation cost. The probe is short and bounded (read the Contract Card; grep the substrate for the inputs it specifies; identify any gap). The result either rules F1 in as a competitive first-surface candidate or routes the foundation work upstream of it.

3. **Reception data from Track A first distribution is not yet at the point where E1's shape is specified.** The first archived distribution shipped 2026-04-28. The Operational Plan v1.1 §8 estimates Phase B at 6–8 weeks. The reception data accumulating over that window directly informs E1's specification — channel choice, archive structure, review-UX needs are downstream of "what does the league actually do with the content?" Picking E1 today specifies the surface from theory; picking E1 in 4–6 weeks specifies it from evidence.

**Recommended follow-on sequencing:**

If reception data is too thin (likely as of 2026-05-10), the diagnostic names that thinness and proposes the Phase B window end as the decision-point.
  - The decision-readiness brief outputs either: (a) a confirmed pick (most likely Axis II / E1, but D1's outcome can promote F1 above it) with a specification-session-ready brief, or (b) a continuing deferral with the named decision-point (likely Phase B window end, ~6–8 weeks).

- **Session after that.** Either the Phase 11 first-surface specification (if the readiness brief resolves the pick) or a continuing deferral that waits on the Phase B window.

- **Concurrent track.** Operational Phase B (Tracks B + D) proceeds on its own cadence per the Operational Plan v1.1, independent of Phase 11 selection-prep. The two tracks converge when the first surface is selected, at which point the surface specification adjudicates the Phase 11 / Phase B layering question explicitly per the §6.1 finding.

**Why this shape and not "just pick E1":** The leading hypothesis is recorded explicitly so the next session does not re-derive it. But the pick is not made because (a) D1 can materially change the candidate set, (b) D2's reception data sharpens E1's specification in ways that matter for the surface specification's quality, and (c) the Phase 11 / Phase B layering question is a real product judgment the founder should make under §9.2 rather than the memo should make under analytical force. The deferral is short — likely one or two doc-only sessions — and produces a specification grounded in evidence rather than theory.

## 10. The point

The Phase 11 first-surface choice is genuine product judgment under doctrinal constraint. The doctrine narrows the field. Three permitted axes remain — historical-archival, consumption-layer, content-class — and the criteria for picking among them do not converge on a single answer.

Axis II (recap-class consumption-layer depth, candidate E1) leads on the most criteria, but the lead is not decisive. Two short diagnostics — Rivalry Chronicle substrate-readiness (D1) and a reception-data re-read (D2) — would substantially narrow the pick. They are the predecessors to whatever session selects.
