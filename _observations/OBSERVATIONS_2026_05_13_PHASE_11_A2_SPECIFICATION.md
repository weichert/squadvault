## 3. Identity and scope

### 3.1 What A2 is

**A2 — Draft History Vault** is a registered Phase 11 product surface presenting a browseable archive of PFL Buddies' auction-draft history across the auction era. The archive surfaces three sub-shapes at v1, each rendered as a per-leaderboard view against canonical events from the substrate's append-only ledger:

- **Auction-Most-Expensive History** — overall record (the single all-time most-expensive pick across the auction era) plus per-position records (the all-time most-expensive RB, WR, QB, TE, K, DEF, and any other position present in the substrate). Per Step 1 §6.2 finding: the per-position view surfaces the Italian Cavallini / Mahomes 2018 anchor pick as the QB-position record, preserving the Voice Profile §5 anchor in a way an overall-only view does not. Lift from D28 (`detect_auction_most_expensive_player_history` at `auction_draft_angles_v1.py:803`) which already computes per-position `pos_max` internally; v1 exposes both the overall record AND the per-position records.
- **Auction-Bust Hall** — top-N cross-era auction picks ranked by combined severity signal `(season_league_starter_avg - player_avg) × bid_amount`, with `starter_weeks >= 4` and `player_avg < season_league_starter_avg × 0.5` as the inclusion filters. Cross-season lift of D22 (`detect_auction_bust` at `auction_draft_angles_v1.py:413`). Step 1 §3.2 confirmed 185 candidates at single-season-D22 thresholds across the 7-season auction substrate.
- **Auction-Bargain Hall** — top-N cross-era auction picks ranked by lowest `bid_amount / total_points` at a minimum-production threshold of `total_points >= 50`. Cross-season lift of D21 (`detect_auction_dollar_per_point` at `auction_draft_angles_v1.py:365`). Step 1 §3.3 confirmed 761 candidates at the production threshold across the auction substrate.

A2's content is **substrate-derivable per D3-Alpha.** Every value displayed traces to a canonical event (`DRAFT_PICK` with `bid_amount` from payload + `WEEKLY_PLAYER_SCORE` for the bust/bargain calculations + `player_directory` for position enrichment) or to an aggregation primitive in `src/squadvault/core/recaps/context/auction_draft_angles_v1.py`. No commissioner-curated labels; no narrative annotation; the substrate facts stand.

A2's **declared temporal scope is the auction era: 2018-2025 with a 2021 gap (7 seasons of auction substrate).** Per Step 1 §4.1 (D2-α): the auction-substrate window is empirically 7 seasons (2018, 2019, 2020, 2022, 2023, 2024, 2025); 2021 has clean `WEEKLY_PLAYER_SCORE` coverage but zero `DRAFT_PICK` events. Cause of the 2021 gap is uncharacterized; founder per session input does not believe the league waiver-format transition (which explains D2-δ FAAB-begins-2021) caused the DRAFT_PICK gap. The pre-2018 era of PFL Buddies' digital history (2010-2017) is auction-substrate-empty per Step 1 §4.2 (D2-β); the league's pre-2010 history is genuine but not substrate-derivable. **The auction era (2018-2025 minus 2021) is A2's declared scope; everything else is out-of-scope at v1.**

### 3.2 What A2 is not

- A2 is **not** the substrate. The substrate (`canonical_events`, `DRAFT_PICK` event payloads, `WEEKLY_PLAYER_SCORE` event payloads, `player_directory` table, the D20-D28 detector suite at `auction_draft_angles_v1.py`) produces the per-(season, franchise, player) facts; A2 reads those facts and presents them as cross-era leaderboards.
- A2 is **not** the artisan-frame. §9.2's artisan-frame is the primary success criterion A2 must pass; A2 is what gets specified.
- A2 is **not** A1. A1 is matchup-derived (`WEEKLY_MATCHUP_RESULT`); A2 is auction-derived (`DRAFT_PICK` + `WEEKLY_PLAYER_SCORE` + `player_directory`). Per A2 selection-prep §4.3, the two substrates are disjoint — a cleaner cluster-A overlap landscape than A1 had with A3.
- A2 is **not** A3 (Championship Timeline). A3 is playoff-bracket-derived (the playoff-detection trick per Architectural Audit §7); no overlap with A2's auction substrate.
- A2 is **not** the Surface Admission Test. Per A1 Reading 1 inheritance, A2 ships as a single Phase 11 surface; the Surface Admission Test's predecessor-state (Roadmap §5.1) is **partially advanced** by A2's registration (three specs now exist post-A2; one-content-class-admission-attempted still gating).

### 3.5 Notable moments triggering push distribution (lands gap #5 from Step 2 §8.3)

**Notable moments** for D4.1-Gamma push distribution are bounded to **three triggers**, each tied to a clear "top of league history" signal (per Step 2 §4.3):

1. **A new top-1 entry in Auction-Most-Expensive History (overall record).** Fires at most once per NFL season at auction night (~late August), when a new pick exceeds the standing all-time top bid amount. Push distribution event references franchise, player, bid amount, and season.
2. **A new top-1 entry in Auction-Bust Hall.** Fires at most once per NFL season at end-of-NFL-season, when full-season production data resolves and a new pick exceeds the standing top-1 bust signal. Push distribution event references franchise, player, bid amount, player average, and season.
3. **A new top-1 entry in Auction-Bargain Hall.** Fires at most once per NFL season at end-of-NFL-season same shape. Push distribution event references franchise, player, bid amount, total points returned, and season.

**Explicitly NOT triggers** (mirroring A1 spec §3.5 prohibitions and Step 2 §4.3 anti-engagement-loop discipline):

- Top-3 / top-5 / top-10 entries (too noisy; would generate a push for every leaderboard change).
- Per-position-record updates (the per-position records under §3.1's most-expensive view; only the overall record fires a push).
- Per-franchise drafting-tendency findings (would belong to §4.2.5; not in v1 trio).
- Position-cost inflation findings (would belong to §4.2.4; not in v1 trio).
- Browse-activity signals (engagement-loop-adjacent; forbidden per §2.3 and §4.4).

**Expected push cadence:** at most 4 events per NFL season under D4.2-Alpha — 1 at auction night (most-expensive update if a new record falls), 0-3 at end-of-NFL-season (bust / bargain updates as production-data resolves). Most NFL seasons will not trigger all events (top-1 thresholds in each leaderboard are relatively stable across most seasons; the all-time most-expensive at $76 has held since 2019; bust and bargain top-1 thresholds may shift more often as production-data resolves).

- **Overall record:** the single all-time most-expensive pick across the auction era.

The Italian Cavallini / Mahomes 2018 anchor pick ($62, franchise 0002, player 9988) lands as the QB-position record under per-position view per Step 1 §3.1 top-10 distribution analysis. This preserves the Voice Profile §5 anchor's visibility in a way the overall-only view does not.

**Top-N value within per-position records is implementation-elected** (recommended: top-1 per position at v1; the spec admits top-3 per position as a defensible expansion that founder may elect at revision-point).

### 4.2 §4.4 — Tone Engine boundary and social-surface vs. social-network

**Tone Engine boundary.** A2 does not modify, extend, or apply post-hoc adjustment to the Tone Engine. **Social-surface vs. social-network.** A2 is a social *surface* — league members view shared auction history through a self-contained archive. A2 is not a social *network*:

- No follower graph, no member-to-member subscription, no opt-in mechanism that creates network effects.
- No comment threads, no member-write-rights, no member-side annotation.

The push distribution events at notable moments (D4.1-Gamma) are **not** an engagement-loop. They are operational signaling that the archive has updated at a top-of-auction-history threshold — analog to E1's distribution events and A1's notable-moment pushes. The bounded-to-three-triggers scope (§3.5) prevents push-cadence drift into engagement-loop territory.

**Confidence: high.** Both tests verifiable from the specified shape.
