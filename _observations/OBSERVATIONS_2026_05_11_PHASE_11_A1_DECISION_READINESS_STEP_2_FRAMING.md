## 3. D3 framing analysis (Decision B — GAF / lore-moments canonicalization)

### 3.1 The three dispositions, operationally

**D3-Alpha — substrate-derivable proxies only.** A1's v1 surfaces only the lore moments derivable from canonical events. "Miller went 1-13" surfaces because the worst-season record displays Miller MGD 2024 at 1-13-0 with PF 1392.75 — the substrate facts the league has decided are lore-moments. The GAF *label* ("The Worst Season," "The Goedert Game") is not stored or surfaced; A1 presents the substrate, and the league supplies the label.

**D3-Beta — commissioner-curated lore annotation layer.** A1 ships substrate-derived moments AND a narrow commissioner-curated annotation table that ties specific (season, week, franchise) tuples to specific league-lore labels. Append-only, commissioner-write, admissible under §8.2 as reference metadata analogous to `nfl_bye_weeks` / `league_scoring_rules` per Narrative_Angles_v2 Dimensions 10-11.

**D3-Gamma — defer the lore-moments dimension entirely.** A1's v1 ships the matchup-derived sub-shapes (championship roll, worst-season tracking, blowouts hall, manager records) and treats GAF / lore as future-session work. The pre-2010 lore dimension is acknowledged as out of scope without an explicit framing layer.

### 3.2 Step 1 evidence

**§6.4 (D3-Alpha-anchoring evidence).** The probe-derived worst-season ladder is dense: Miller 1-13 (2024), Brandon 0-14 (2025), Miller 2-11 (2017), Ben's Gods 2-11 (2011), Eddie 3-11 (2022), Cavallini 3-11 (2021), plus multiple 3-10 seasons. The Voice Profile §5 *"the league remembers mistakes and brings them back up at the worst possible moment — affectionately"* pattern is substantively present at the empirical bottom of the records distribution. Substrate-derivable proxies are dense enough to carry significant editorial load without GAF annotation.

**§6.1 (1983-inception ramification).** Pre-2010 league memory is GAF-shaped by definition: real, culturally-significant, not substrate-derivable. The league has ~27 pre-digital seasons. D3-Gamma's cost is asymmetrically raised by this finding (anti-drift §9): ignoring the densest GAF-shaped content the league has is harder to justify post-Step-1 than it was pre-Step-1. D3-Beta's potential benefit is narrower than first reading suggests, because most pre-2010 lore *cannot* be substrate-anchored even under D3-Beta — there's no canonical (season, week, franchise) tuple to attach a label to. The Voice Profile §4 entry for "The Ostrich" — *"a specific legendary night at the cabin. Referenced but never fully explained — inside joke"* — is the load-bearing test case: a pre-2010 GAF moment that even commissioner annotation cannot surface, because the substrate has no row to annotate.

### 3.3 Doctrinal-fit considerations

**Reset Memo §2.3 silence over speculation.** D3-Alpha is the §2.3-pure shape: A1 presents canonical facts; the league supplies the cultural overlay. D3-Beta is admissible-in-principle (the commissioner is human, the annotation is approved by humans, the narrative isn't fact-creating because the underlying record is canonical) but is operationally closer to the speculation boundary — once labels enter the system, the question "is this label what the league actually calls this moment, or is it the commissioner's read of what they call it?" becomes operative.

**Voice Profile §6.** *"Use the league's natural vocabulary where it emerges from the data ... without forcing terms the system hasn't earned."* This is anti-D3-Beta in spirit at the v1 timing. The substrate has not yet surfaced enough of the canonical proxies to know which labels emerge naturally vs. which are commissioner-imposed. Re-read at a revision-point — *after* observing how the league talks about A1's substrate-derivable presentations — would be the moment to evaluate whether D3-Beta admission makes sense.

**Voice Profile §7.** Explicitly not authorized: *"Fabricating group chat quotes, reactions, or inside jokes not present in canonical data."* D3-Beta walks close to the line. The narrowest D3-Beta — "Miller's Genuine Draft 2024 1-13 → 'The Worst Season'" — arguably stays inside the line because the underlying record is canonical and the label is the commissioner's act, not the system's fabrication. But the line is real, and the §7 prohibition is doctrinally binding.

**Reset Memo §8.2 admissibility (the parallel to nfl_bye_weeks / league_scoring_rules).** D3-Beta as reference metadata is admissible-in-principle per the selection-prep §6.3 finding: a narrow table of (season, week, franchise, label) rows is structurally analogous to the Narrative_Angles_v2 Dimensions 10-11 precedent. **The admissibility-in-principle stands;** Step 2 does not foreclose D3-Beta as a future expansion path.

**Parent §4 Cluster B failure-mode patterns.** Cluster B failed §8.2 on substantial new foundations: ingest pipelines, storage layers, governance contracts. D3-Beta's annotation table is structurally much narrower — no ingest pipeline, minimal storage, governance is one human (the commissioner) appending rows. The framing call is closer to Cluster A's pass than to Cluster B's failure. But the *§4.4 social-surface boundary* tension flagged in selection-prep §10 side-finding #1 is real: once A1 publishes commissioner-imposed labels, the question of whether A1 is presenting league history vs. commissioner editorializing about league history becomes operative.

### 3.4 Downstream implications of each disposition

**D3-Alpha downstream.** A1's v1 ships only with substrate fields rendered. Spec session does not design an annotation table or admission mechanism for labels. Voice copy at the surface is descriptive ("Miller's Genuine Draft, 2024 — 1-13-0") without commissioner labeling. The pre-2010 lore gap is addressed by *framing* at the copy level: A1 declares its scope honestly ("the digital era / since 2010 / the MFL years"). This is the smallest spec-session footprint.

**D3-Beta downstream.** Spec session must specify the annotation table schema (columns, write-rights, append-only invariants), the governance for commissioner annotation (which rows get labels; what labels are admissible; how labels render), and the §8.4 doctrinal-fit certification that the annotation does not cross the §4.4 social-surface boundary. This is a non-trivial spec-session expansion. The marginal §9.2 fit gain is bounded — most pre-2010 lore cannot be annotated (per the Ostrich finding); post-2010 labels mostly relabel records the substrate already surfaces (per Step 1 §6.4 density).

**D3-Gamma downstream.** A1's v1 is matchup-derived only. No framing copy at the digital-era boundary; pre-2010 is silent rather than explicitly out of scope. This is the most-minimalist v1 but is structurally weaker than D3-Alpha post-Step-1: silence on the 1983-inception finding leaves the temporal-scope question implicit when Step 1 made it explicit.

### 3.5 Disposition: **D3-Alpha**

D3-Alpha for v1, with explicit framing copy at the digital-era boundary acknowledging that the substrate captures 2010 onward and the league predates the archive by ~27 years. The D3-Beta admissibility-in-principle finding stands as a doctrinally-admissible future-expansion path; not exercised at v1.

**Reasoning:**

1. Step 1 §6.4 anchors D3-Alpha empirically. Substrate density is sufficient to carry the §9.2 GAF-pattern editorial weight without annotation.
2. Voice Profile §6 argues against forcing commissioner-imposed labels before observing which labels emerge naturally from the substrate-derivable presentations.
3. Voice Profile §7 explicit prohibition is doctrinally binding; D3-Beta walks closer to the line than D3-Alpha.
4. D3-Beta's marginal benefit is narrower than first reading suggests — the Ostrich finding establishes a class of GAF moments that no annotation can surface (the pre-2010 unanchored lore). Most pre-2010 lore is in this class.
5. D3-Beta admissibility-in-principle is preserved as future expansion; D3-Alpha is *path-compatible* with later D3-Beta admission. Choosing D3-Alpha now does not foreclose D3-Beta later.
6. D3-Alpha addresses Step 1 §6.1 by *framing* (declared scope: "the digital era"), not by *silence* (D3-Gamma).

**Alternative the founder may elect (D3-Beta):** anchored on the §8.2 admissibility-in-principle finding from selection-prep §6.3, on the Voice Profile §5 GAF pattern being the highest §9.2 fit content A1 could ship, and on a judgment that commissioner-curated labels are "the system earning the term" rather than "importing an inside joke." Defensible. If elected, the spec session designs the narrow annotation table per §8.2 reference-metadata pattern; the §4.4 social-surface boundary call is part of spec-session §8.4 certification.

**Why the founder elects:** Step 1 §6.4 (D3-Alpha-anchoring) and Step 1 §6.1 (D3-Beta-leaning, via the pre-2010 lore gap) point opposite directions. The two pieces of evidence are roughly equal in weight, and the resolution depends on whether the founder reads commissioner-curated labels as fabricating-or-earning. The chat session lands D3-Alpha as recommended; the founder elects.

**Confidence:** **Medium-high.** The disposition is well-anchored on Step 1 + Voice Profile; the alternative is admissible-and-defensible; the call is genuinely under-determined at the chat-session level.

## 4. D4 framing analysis (Decision C — channel + cadence anchor)

### 4.1 D4.1 channel — the three options

**D4.1-Alpha (distribute through E1's channel at notable moments only).** A1 is push-only — distribution events fire when notable updates land (a new championship; a new worst-season; a new top-1 in any leaderboard). No browseable archive; A1's content arrives, is consumed in the channel, and lives in the channel's history.

**D4.1-Beta (separately-accessible archive, browse-only).** A1 ships as a git-backed markdown collection (analog to E1's `_observations/`-resident pattern) or static web page or markdown export. League members visit when they choose. No push distribution.

**D4.1-Gamma (both — archive-resident with push distribution at notable moments).** A1's primary artifact is the archive; the operational mechanism at notable moments is a push distribution event through E1's channel that points to the archive. The archive is what A1 *is*; the push is what A1 *does* when substrate accumulation crosses a notable threshold.

### 4.2 D4.1 considerations

**Step 1 §6.5 (recency-clustering).** Brandon 2025 appears as the worst-season top-1 (0-14-0) and the losing side of the blowouts top-1 (Paradis 161.40 over Brandon 54.80, W14 2025, margin 106.60). End of 2025 season is a natural push moment under D4.1-Alpha or D4.1-Gamma — two leaderboards updating in the same direction simultaneously. D4.1-Beta would defer to whenever league members happen to browse.

**Step 1 §6.2 (format-shift comparability).** Cross-era comparability is a display-side concern (how the leaderboards render), not a channel-side concern. D4.1 disposition is roughly orthogonal to §6.2.

**Voice Profile §5 "Piling On."** The league pattern is to bring up mistakes at notable moments. A1 surfacing "the all-time worst season has been updated: Brandon 0-14 (2025)" at end-of-season *is* the league pattern. D4.1-Alpha and D4.1-Gamma honor this; D4.1-Beta is silent at the cultural moment.

**§4.4 social-surface boundary.** D4.1-Alpha (push only) is the most network-adjacent shape: push distribution without a self-contained archive creates a thinner social-surface posture (A1 is what happens *in the channel*, not a thing-in-itself). D4.1-Beta is the most surface-pure shape: A1 is a self-contained thing; league members find it. D4.1-Gamma is in the middle: the archive is the thing; the push is operational signaling that the thing has updated.

**E1 infrastructure inheritance.** D4.1-Alpha and D4.1-Gamma ride E1's existing channel; no new infrastructure. D4.1-Beta requires the archive infrastructure to be designed (markdown collection? static page? something else?), which is spec-session scope but is light per the parent §4 Cluster A "Pass" on §8.2 (pure consumer; no new ingest paths).

### 4.3 D4.1 disposition: **D4.1-Gamma**

Archive-resident with push distribution at notable moments through E1's channel. Recommended because:

1. The archive is the *substantive* artifact A1 is — a browseable historical archive matches parent §4's framing ("browseable league history is exactly the kind of surface a longtime fantasy football league would actually enjoy").
2. Push at notable moments honors Voice Profile §5's "Piling On" pattern — the league does bring up mistakes at notable moments; A1 surfacing those moments operationally is the league pattern, not an engagement loop.
3. D4.1-Gamma is path-compatible with both alternatives: if push events turn out to feel like engagement-loop-adjacent (a revision-point finding), the operational mechanism can be removed and A1 falls back to D4.1-Beta cleanly. If the archive-resident pattern turns out to feel disconnected from league use (a different revision-point finding), the archive can become subordinate to the push mechanism and A1 falls back to D4.1-Alpha.
4. Reading-1 + D4.1-Gamma is a coherent shape: A1 is a single surface with one channel (the archive) and one operational mechanism (push at notable moments); content-class admission is downstream of the Surface Admission Test.

**Spec-session implication:** D4.1-Gamma requires the spec session to define *what counts as a notable moment* (a new championship? A new top-N in any leaderboard? A specific threshold like a new all-time blowout?). This is a bounded spec decision; the framing is well-grounded in Step 1 §6.5 recency-clustering plus Voice Profile §5.

**Alternatives:** D4.1-Beta (archive-only) is the minimalist alternative; defensible if the founder reads push events at notable moments as engagement-loop-adjacent. D4.1-Alpha (push-only) is the operational-momentum alternative; defensible if the founder reads the archive as overkill for the v1 substrate density.

**Confidence:** **Medium.** Lower than B because the operational-shape call is genuinely judgment-under-uncertainty; the founder's read of the league's relationship to push-vs-browse content may differ.

**D4.2-Alpha (NFL Week 1 ~2026-09-08, shared with E1).** Cross-surface consistency. Selection-prep §8.2 leading hypothesis under the "shared calendar, different substance" pattern: A1's revision-point fires at the same moment E1's does, but evaluates different questions.

**D4.2-Gamma (auction-draft moment ~late August).** PFL-specific. The league actively re-engages with historical context for the new season at the cabin draft weekend.

### 4.5 D4.2 considerations

**Cross-surface consistency vs. substance-honesty.** E1 spec §8 elected NFL W1 over the Phase B window-end on substance-honesty grounds — *"more honest anchor for voice-iteration work."* For E1, NFL W1 is the substantively right moment because that's when live cadence reception starts accumulating. For A1, NFL W1 is the cross-surface-consistent moment, but the substantively right moment for end-of-season-accumulating data is end-of-season. **The structural analogy to E1's election inverts.** Cross-surface consistency on the *anchor* preserves "shared calendar, different substance" coherence; substance-honesty would elect a different anchor.

**Step 1 §6.2 (2021 format-shift).** Championship lands W16 pre-2021 vs W18 post-2021. End-of-season is not a fixed calendar moment; it's an event-anchored moment. D4.2-Beta would specify by event ("when the championship is decided"), not by calendar date. D4.2-Alpha avoids this complication by anchoring on NFL W1, which IS a fixed calendar moment.

**Step 1 §6.5 (recency-clustering).** End-of-season is when new substrate lands; that's when the leaderboards update. Under D4.2-Alpha, the *content updates* land at end-of-season (operational-substrate-side; not anchored), and the *revision-point* fires months later at NFL W1. Under D4.2-Beta, content updates and revision-point coincide. Either is operationally workable; D4.2-Alpha separates content-update from spec-revision more clearly.

**Selection-prep §8.2 (the niche-generality framing).** *"League-specific cadence anchors should be parameterizable, not hardcoded."* D4.2-Gamma (auction-draft) couples A1's spec to a PFL Buddies-specific tradition in a way that may not generalize across leagues. D4.2-Alpha and D4.2-Beta both anchor on NFL calendar moments which generalize.

**Revision-point vs. content-update separation.** The revision-point per E1 spec §8 is the moment to evaluate whether the *specification* needs structural revision; it's not the same as the moment when *content* updates land. For A1, the substrate-side content update (Brandon 2025 entering the leaderboards) is an operational event that happens at end-of-season regardless of when the revision-point fires. D4.2-Alpha cleanly separates these: content updates at end-of-season (operationally event-driven); revision-point at NFL W1 (evaluates whether the spec, surfaced over an inter-season period plus initial weeks of the next season, needs structural revision).

NFL Week 1 ~2026-09-08, shared with E1, per selection-prep §8.2 leading hypothesis. Recommended because:

## 6. Surface-vs-meta-surface adjudication (Decision A — the pivotal call)

Per selection-prep §5: Reading 1 (A1 as single surface) was named as the default by structural analogy to E1's §9.2 election (which elected the minimalist reading), but Reading 2 (A1 as meta-surface) was explicitly preserved as admissible-and-defensible. Step 2's job is to either confirm the Reading 1 default with explicit reasoning or elect Reading 2 with explicit reasoning. Reading-1-by-inertia is not a valid Step 2 output; Reading-2-by-novelty-bias is not either.

### 6.1 The two readings under Step 1 evidence

**Reading 1 (A1 as single surface).** A1's first ship is a Phase 11 surface like E1 — one surface, with one (or a small set of) content type(s), that admits later expansion through subsequent specifications. Concretely: A1 ships championship roll + worst-season tracking + blowouts hall as v1; A2 (Draft History Vault) and A3 (Championship Timeline) sequence as separate within-cluster successors; further content classes admit via the Surface Admission Test when it ships as its own framework artifact.

**Reading 2 (A1 as meta-surface).** A1 is specified as a *category* of surface that admits multiple sub-shapes (content classes) governed by a shared specification with per-sub-shape invariants. A2 and A3 are reclassified as A1 content classes rather than as separate surfaces. A1's v1 ships a starter set of content classes; subsequent content classes admit via a content-admission mechanism that either *is* the Surface Admission Test (D5-Alpha) or is a lighter mechanism A1 specifies for itself (D5-Beta).

### 6.2 Considerations weighed

**Step 1 §6.3 (manager-records tenure-policy fork) under each reading.** The empirical magnitude (5 of top-10 pairs transformed by 13-27 meetings; Italian Cavallini / Brandon Knows Ball goes from 18-10-1 in 29 meetings all-time to 1-2 in 3 meetings tenure-scoped) is larger than selection-prep §4.2.4 anticipated. Under Reading 1, the tenure-policy disposition is a spec-internal call A1's spec session makes once. Under Reading 2, tenure-policy is a content-class governance question that interacts with the meta-surface's admission mechanism — does each content class get its own tenure-policy, or do they share one? Reading 1 is operationally simpler for the empirical magnitude; Reading 2 creates a new governance dimension (per-content-class policy) that the magnitude does not obviously require.

**Step 1 §6.1 (1983-inception) under each reading.** Under Reading 1, A1 ships as "the digital era archive" with copy-level honesty about the 2010 boundary and the league's 27 pre-digital seasons. The temporal-scope framing is bounded and singular.
