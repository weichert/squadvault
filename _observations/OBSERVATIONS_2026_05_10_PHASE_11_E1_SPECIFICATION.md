### 4.2 §4.4 — Tone Engine boundary and social-surface vs social-network

**Tone Engine boundary.** E1 consumes the recap artifact's narrative verbatim. The narrative was produced by the substrate's creative layer using the Tone Engine and the PFL Buddies Voice Profile upstream of E1. E1 does not modify the Tone Engine, does not add a new tone, does not add a tone selector, does not apply post-hoc tone smoothing. Tone iteration (Track C in Operational Plan §8) is engine-increment work, not surface work; if a future tone iteration ships, E1 consumes the engine's new output without modification. The boundary is preserved by the architectural constraint that E1's only input is the approved artifact's shareable parts.

**Social-surface vs social-network.** E1 is a social *surface* — league members receive shared content through a channel they already use. E1 is not a social *network* — there is no follower graph, no engagement loop, no opt-in mechanism that creates network effects, no friend-of-friend, no recommendation, no public discoverability. The channel is the league's existing group-text thread, with a fixed and small membership the league itself maintains. The archive is a git-backed markdown directory; it has no member-facing UX, no access management, no read-tracking. The social fabric exists in the league; E1 delivers content into that fabric. The fabric is not constructed by E1.

**Confidence: high.** Both tests are verifiable from the code (`scripts/distribute_recap.py` — read-only DB access, paste-assist channel, no engine modification) and from the operational facts (single shared channel, no follower graph).

## 6. Surface-specific invariants

E1's invariants beyond the substrate's invariants:

### 6.1 Distribution requires APPROVED-state artifact

No DRAFT-state distribution, ever. The script's gate enforces this. The gate has caught a real edge case at W7 v27 (latest version DRAFT, prior APPROVED orphaned). The behavior is: refuse distribution; print the version state; require human action to resolve. The "human action to resolve" includes approval of the latest DRAFT, regeneration via creative layer, or WITHHOLD with a forward-carried plan to re-approve a prior version (per Track A shipping memo F5; lifecycle-action gap flagged for future revision).

### 6.2 Archive entry created in lockstep with distribution

The archive entry and the distribution event are produced atomically by the distribution script. One cannot exist without the other. The archive is the canonical record of what was distributed; there is no separate distribution log.

The atomicity is operationally important because the archive frontmatter is the source of truth for distribution provenance (channel, recipient, fingerprint, timestamp). A distribution event without an archive entry would be invisible to the project's history; an archive entry without a distribution event would be a fiction.

### 6.3 Reception capture is voice-iteration data, not engagement data

Binding per Operational Plan §10 commitment #4. The constraint applies to every component of E1 that touches reception:

- Reception observations record the *content* of replies, reactions, and references (their gist; their tone; their content engagement). They do not record metrics about replies (count, time-to-first-reply, member-coverage-rate, silent-vs-vocal ratio).
- Reception analysis (in retrospectives per Operational Plan §9.3) evaluates voice fit and consumption-layer signal quality, not engagement rate. Member-by-member breakdowns are limited to voice-relevant observations (e.g., "kent's response to long-form prose has been consistently positive"); they do not constitute audience segmentation.
- Reception observations may include silence-as-finding (no replies in a window). Silence is signal about the league's relationship to a specific piece of content, not a failure metric and not an input to optimization.

This invariant is preserved by what the spec **forbids E1 to do**, not by what the spec instructs E1 to do. The forbidden operations include: engagement-rate calculation, member-by-member retention tracking, content recommendation, A/B testing, scheduling optimization, audience segmentation, growth-loop construction.

### 6.4 No follower graph, no engagement loop, no opt-in network mechanism

Binding per Reset Memo §4.4 (social-surface-vs-social-network distinction). E1 ships content into the league's existing communication infrastructure (the group text thread). Membership of that thread is governed by the league itself, by its own conventions, on its own timetable. E1 does not maintain a list of recipients, does not provide opt-in / opt-out for the system level (the league member's relationship to the thread is the league's affair), does not surface "who read what," does not enable subscriptions, does not construct a community around the artifact.

### 6.5 Append-only at every surface

Reception YAML: append-only. Archive markdown: append-only. Distribution events recorded in artifact frontmatter: append-only. The `recap_artifacts` table: append-only (the DRAFT/APPROVED/SUPERSEDED state machine adds rows; it does not edit existing rows). The `editorial_actions` ledger: append-only.

This is the substrate's foundational invariant per Reset Memo §2.3 first principle. E1 honors it by inheritance, not by separate mechanism. Any future operational-mechanism gap (e.g., the Track A F5 "promote prior APPROVED forward" semantic) resolves *under* append-only, by adding a new row, not by mutating existing rows.

### 6.6 Channel and content-class admissibility

E1 currently admits **one channel** (`group_text_paste_assist`) and **one content class** (`weekly_recap_v1` artifact-kind). Expanding either is a revision-point decision per §8 of this memo, not a default. The expansion mechanism is:

- **Channel expansion** (e.g., adding email or MFL message): revision-point decision under §8; the expansion may require a new section in the distribution runbook and a per-channel paste-preview implementation. The mechanism is operational-judgment, anchored on the per-channel fit test.
- **Content-class expansion** (e.g., admitting Rivalry Chronicle v1 to distribute through E1): governed by the Surface Admission Test framework artifact (deferred to its own session per Reset Memo §10.2). Until that framework artifact ships, content-class expansion is not admitted to E1's spec.

### 6.7 Specification governs the live baseline; live baseline does not silently revise specification

The live baseline as registered in §5 is the spec's reference shape. If operational work changes the baseline (e.g., the runbook gains a new step, or the script gains a new gate), the change either (a) is consistent with the spec and the spec stands without amendment, or (b) is inconsistent with the spec and either the spec amends or the change reverts. Silent divergence between operational reality and registered specification is a finding; it does not resolve by either side defaulting.

The revision-point per §8 is the natural moment to surface and resolve any accumulated divergence.

**Confidence on invariants: high.** Each invariant is grounded in operational evidence or in a binding doctrinal section verified per §2.
