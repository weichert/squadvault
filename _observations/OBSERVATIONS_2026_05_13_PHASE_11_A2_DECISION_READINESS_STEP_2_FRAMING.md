### 4.2 D4.2 — cadence anchor (the load-bearing call)

Three candidate anchors per selection-prep §6.4 / §8.2:

- **D4.2-Alpha** — NFL Week 1 (~2026-09-08), shared with E1 and A1. Cross-surface consistency.
- **D4.2-Beta** — End-of-NFL-season (~February 2027), substance-complete moment for end-of-season production-data resolution.
- **D4.2-Gamma** — Auction-draft moment (~late August), substantively-novel A2-specific anchor where new substrate accumulation happens.

#### Substance-honesty argument for D4.2-Gamma

A2's substrate-side update rhythm is two-phase: auction night (new `DRAFT_PICK` events) + end-of-season (production-data resolves bust/bargain leaderboards). **The auction-night moment is where A2's substrate genuinely accumulates new content** — fresh picks land, fresh records get set, the most-expensive leaderboard potentially gets a new top-1 entry. Anchoring A2's revision-point on auction night is substance-honest in a way that anchoring on NFL Week 1 (a few weeks downstream of auction night) is not. For A2 specifically, the auction-night anchor is more substantively-relevant than the analogous PFL cabin-draft-weekend anchor was for A1 (which had no analogous substrate-accumulation moment).

#### Stability and cross-surface-consistency arguments for D4.2-Alpha

Three considerations weigh toward Alpha:

1. **Cross-surface consistency.** E1 spec §8 and A1 spec §8 both anchor on NFL Week 1 under the "shared calendar, different substance" pattern. A2 anchoring on NFL Week 1 means one calendar moment per year for all Phase 11 revision-points; the spec-revision discipline is unified.

2. **The 2021-gap stability interaction (Step 1 §6.6).** A2's substrate has a confirmed 2021 gap (Step 1 §4.1; cause uncharacterized — three candidates: snake-format-2021, different event_type, ingest gap). Under D4.2-Gamma, the 2021 revision-point either does not fire (no auction occurred for the league in that format), or fires on a different-format moment whose semantics we cannot characterize without resolving the cause. Under D4.2-Alpha, the 2021 revision-point fires normally against whatever substrate state exists at NFL Week 1 2022 (which simply lacks 2021 auction picks). **The unresolved 2021-cause argues marginally for Alpha on stability grounds.**

3. **The Compact's niche-generality framing.** Auction-night is a PFL-specific tradition (cabin draft weekend in late August per Voice Profile §1). Using it as a revision-point anchor couples A2's spec to one league's calendar. NFL Week 1 is a generally-applicable anchor across any fantasy football league running on a US NFL calendar. The substance-honesty gain for D4.2-Gamma comes with a niche-generality cost.

#### Counter-consideration on the 2021-gap stability argument

The 2021-cause has substrate-context that strengthens characterization: per founder input this session, the league transitioned waiver formats around the 2021 boundary (from fixed-waiver-order to FAAB-auction-style waivers — directly explains D2-δ FAAB-begins-2021). The waiver-format transition is **not** the cause of the DRAFT_PICK gap per founder disposition, but the 2021 boundary clearly carried other league-format work; the DRAFT_PICK gap may be related (one-year draft-format experiment? format-transition collateral?) even if not directly caused by the waiver transition. **The 2021 boundary is a known league-format-shift moment; the DRAFT_PICK gap is one of several format-related substrate signals at that boundary.** This contextualizes but does not resolve the gap.

#### Disposition: **D4.2-Alpha** (recommended; founder may elect Gamma)

A2's revision-point anchors on NFL Week 1 (~2026-09-08), shared with E1 and A1 under the "shared calendar, different substance" pattern.

**Reasoning:**

1. Cross-surface consistency with E1 and A1 (one calendar moment for all Phase 11 revisions).
2. Stability under the unresolved 2021 gap.
3. Niche-generality cost of D4.2-Gamma is real (auction-night is PFL-specific).
4. The substance-honesty argument for D4.2-Gamma is acknowledged and is the genuinely-defensible alternative; the chat-session recommendation lands on Alpha grounded on (1)-(3) but the founder's product judgment may legitimately elect Gamma if substance-honesty is weighted more highly than cross-surface-consistency.

**The alternative the founder may elect (D4.2-Gamma):** anchored on auction-night substance-honesty; A2's revision-point fires when new auction substrate genuinely lands. If elected, A2's spec opens with a niche-generality flag (the anchor is league-calendar-specific and must be parameterizable at niche-generalization time) and the 2021 revision-point treatment is a spec-internal call (skip the year; fire on alternative-format moment; or other).

**Why the founder elects:** D4.2 is the load-bearing call this session surfaces. Selection-prep §8.2 named it. Step 1 §6.6 surfaced the 2021-gap interaction. This memo lands the recommended default with the reasoning; the founder elects per §9.2 product judgment.

**Confidence:** **Medium-high.** Reasoning is anchored on three considerations toward Alpha; substance-honesty argument for Gamma is real; founder's election is product judgment under §9.2.
