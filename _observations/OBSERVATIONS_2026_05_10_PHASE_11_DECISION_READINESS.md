### 2.4 New finding — Operational Plan §8 Phase B duration drift

While re-reading the Operational Plan v1.1 §8 for D2, a separate drift surfaced that the parent memo did not flag and that the session brief inherited.

**Operational Plan v1.1 §8 source text at line 462** (verified via direct file inspection in /mnt/project/SquadVault_Operational_Plan_v1_1.md):

> ### Phase B — Reception capture and operational rhythm (Tracks B + D)
> **Duration:** 4–8 weeks (~12–24 hours total commissioner work plus ongoing cadence work)
> **Deliverables:** Reception capture mechanism, edit-diff capture, scheduler wiring, Wednesday digest, six consecutive weekly cycles run on schedule.
> **Exit criteria:** Six weeks of automated rhythm. Reception data accumulating. Commissioner time stabilizing toward 1–3 hours/week.

**Drift propagation:**

- Parent memo `5a865a1` §6.3 quotes: *"The Operational Plan v1.1 §8 estimates Phase B at 6–8 weeks."* — **incorrect**, source says **4–8 weeks.**
- MVP closure memo `OBSERVATIONS_2026_05_02_MVP_PER_IRP_COMPLETE.md` "What's next per the plan" quotes: *"The plan's framing is '6–8 weeks…'"* — **incorrect**, source says 4–8 weeks.
- This session's brief "Decision-Point Calendar" carries the same misquote: *"Operational Plan §8 Phase B window estimate: 6–8 weeks. Phase B window-end range: 2026-06-09 (6 weeks) to 2026-06-23 (8 weeks)."* — **lower bound is incorrect.**

**Honest reading of the source:**

- **Duration estimate** (wall-clock work): 4–8 weeks
- **Exit criteria** for Phase B: "Six weeks of automated rhythm" — i.e., six weekly cycles, not six wall-clock weeks
- **Track B exit criteria** (separately, at §8.b in Track B description): "After 4–6 distributed pieces, the commissioner has structured observation of what the league responded to and what fell flat."

The likely origin of the drift: someone conflated the "six weeks of automated rhythm" exit criterion with the duration estimate, producing "6 weeks" as a lower bound. This is a substantive misreading because the exit criteria is a *cycle count* (which can elapse faster or slower than wall-clock weeks) while the duration is an *elapsed-time estimate.*

**Corrected decision-point calendar** (replacing the session brief's): Track A first distribution 2026-04-28; Phase B duration window-end ranges from **2026-05-26 (4 weeks)** to **2026-06-23 (8 weeks)**. If the relevant signal is "six weekly cycles," the wall-clock anchor depends on distribution cadence, not wall-clock weeks.

**Additional caveat — inter-season cadence question.** PFL Buddies' 2025 NFL season concluded in January 2026. The W7 distribution on 2026-04-28 is months *post-season.* The next live weekly cycle is 2026 NFL Week 1, approximately early September 2026 — well past the 8-week wall-clock window. The "six weekly cycles" exit criterion thus interacts with the founder's decision to either (a) backfill distribute archived recaps from earlier seasons during the inter-season period to accelerate cycle accumulation, or (b) wait for the 2026 NFL season to start the live weekly cadence. Neither is forced; both are operational choices.

**Disposition:** record the finding here; do not patch the parent memo or the MVP closure memo (append-only, no silent revision). The corrected calendar applies to this memo's §5 disposition. A future Operational Plan re-read may want to clarify the duration-vs-exit-criteria distinction in the source itself; that is its own session.

Confidence: **high** (verified by direct file inspection).
