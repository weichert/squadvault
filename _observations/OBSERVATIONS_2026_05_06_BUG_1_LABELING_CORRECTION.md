## Mechanism re-derivation

Reading `weekly_recap_lifecycle.py:773–786`:

```python
if _all_angles:
    budgeted = []
    h_count = n_count = 0
    minor_pool: list[NarrativeAngle] = []
    for a in _all_angles:
        if a.strength >= 3 and h_count < 3:        # HEADLINE pass
            budgeted.append(a)
            h_count += 1
        elif a.strength == 2 and n_count < 6:       # NOTABLE pass
            budgeted.append(a)
            n_count += 1
        elif a.strength <= 1:
            minor_pool.append(a)                    # MINOR pool
```

The strength gate is unambiguous:

- **HEADLINE pass:** only `strength >= 3` angles eligible. T3
  strength-3 STREAK angles compete here.
- **NOTABLE pass:** only `strength == 2` angles eligible.
  T9-LOSS strength-2 angles compete here (post-§10 Q1).
- **MINOR pool:** only `strength <= 1` angles eligible.

A strength=2 T9-LOSS angle structurally cannot be evicted from
HEADLINE — it never enters the HEADLINE pass to begin with. The
`_all_angles` list is sorted by `(-strength, category, headline)`
at line 760, so all strength-3 angles are processed before any
strength-2 angle is even considered. The HEADLINE budget filling
or saturating affects T3 strength-3 STREAK; it does not affect
T9-LOSS strength-2.

id=142's mechanism is **NOTABLE saturation**: the angles block
shows exactly 6 NOTABLE entries (cap reached). The strength=2
T9-LOSS angle competed for one of those 6 slots and lost via
the alphabetical tiebreak in the existing sort. With NOTABLE
full and the angle's strength below the MINOR pool's threshold
(strength=2 is not `<= 1`), the angle had no remaining path to
the rendered block. It joined the "112 minor angles omitted"
count.

The error mechanism:

- The §10 Q1 closure memo at `a5c5c1b` was written immediately
  after observing id=142's "112 minor angles omitted" footer.
  The framing reasoned from "Bug 1 is HEADLINE budget eviction

  source. A strength=2 angle cannot enter HEADLINE. Reading the

  during the specimen-2 hunt session. By the time the
  cross-franchise scan was running, "Bug 1 = HEADLINE budget
  eviction at strength=2" had become assumed truth in the

## Methodological note (carry-forward)

The lesson formalized: **before promoting a finding into a
named thread, derive the mechanism against current source
code.** This applies even when the finding seems to match a
named existing thread — the matching might be on a surface
phrase ("budget eviction") rather than mechanism (HEADLINE pass
vs NOTABLE pass). The cost of re-derivation is a few minutes;
the cost of mis-labeling is downstream confusion that propagates
into briefs and design work.

Self-critique on whether the original Step 0b memo's framing
contributed to the error: the Step 0b memo was correct — it
explicitly named "HEADLINE budget revision" and tied the fix
to "high-`|streak|` T3" eviction. Today's memos generalized
that framing to a different angle (T9-LOSS strength-2) without
checking whether the mechanism still applied. The error was in
generalization, not in the original framing.
