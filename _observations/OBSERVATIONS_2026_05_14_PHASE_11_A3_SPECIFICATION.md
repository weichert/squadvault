## 3. Identity and scope

### 3.1 What A3 is

**A3 — Championship Timeline** is a registered Phase 11 product surface presenting a browseable archive of PFL Buddies' playoff history across the digital era. The archive surfaces three sub-shapes at v1, each rendered against canonical `WEEKLY_MATCHUP_RESULT` events from the substrate's append-only ledger via the playoff-detection trick:

- **Per-Season Playoff Bracket** — for each digital-era season, the full playoff bracket: who played whom across all playoff rounds (preliminary round, semifinal, championship), scores, advance vs eliminate. Generalizes `compute_championship_roll` (`hall_of_fame_aggregations_v1.py:234`) to emit all playoff-week matchups per season rather than narrowing to the championship-week singleton. Per Step 1 §4.3 / §6.1: the post-2021 W17/W18 duplicate championship rows are collapsed by content at the derivation layer (see §3.6 below). **Cross-links to A1's `championship_roll.md` for the championship-week row** — A3 surfaces the rounds A1 does not (per the selection-prep §3.1 absorption-finding boundary; A3 does not re-render A1's championship-roll content).
- **Cross-Season Playoff Records** — all-time leaderboards across the 16-season digital era: per-franchise playoff-season appearances; per-franchise championship-matchup appearances; longest playoff-active streak; longest playoff-drought streak. Cross-season lift of D39 (`detect_championship_history` at `franchise_deep_angles_v1.py:1248`) which already operates at cross-season scope within its playoff-week branch. Per Step 1 §6.3: the lift uses **per-season set semantics** for playoff-appearance counts, NOT D39's internal per-matchup `playoff_appearances` over-counting (see §3.8 below).
- **Bridesmaids** — cross-era championship-runner-up leaderboard: franchises that appeared as the championship-matchup loser, counted across all 16 digital-era seasons. Derived from `compute_championship_roll`'s `runner_up_id` column aggregated by franchise. Per Step 1 §3.3 / §6.2: the v1 sub-shape ships the **runner-up leg only**; the "one-game-out-of-playoffs" almost leg (D50 `detect_the_almost`) is dropped from v1 on substrate-thinness grounds (see §3.7 below).

A3's content is **substrate-derivable per D3-Alpha.** Every value displayed traces to a canonical `WEEKLY_MATCHUP_RESULT` event or to an aggregation primitive in `src/squadvault/core/recaps/context/hall_of_fame_aggregations_v1.py` and `franchise_deep_angles_v1.py`. No commissioner-curated labels; no narrative annotation; the substrate facts stand.

A3's **declared temporal scope is the digital era: 2010-2025 (16 seasons, complete; no gaps).** Per Step 1 §4.1 (D2-α): `WEEKLY_MATCHUP_RESULT` coverage is empirically complete across all 16 seasons — 1,182 matchups, no inter-season gaps, no intra-season week gaps. This is the most-mature canonical event substrate of the three cluster-A surfaces (A1 inherited the same completeness; A2's auction substrate had a 2021 gap). The league's pre-2010 history is genuine but not substrate-derivable. **The digital era (2010-2025) is A3's declared scope; the pre-2010 era is out-of-scope at v1.**

### 3.2 What A3 is not

- A3 is **not** the substrate. The substrate (`canonical_events`, `WEEKLY_MATCHUP_RESULT` event payloads, the playoff-detection trick at `_regular_season_matchup_count` + matchup-count filter) produces the per-(season, week, matchup) facts; A3 reads those facts and presents them as bracket views and cross-era leaderboards.
- A3 is **not** the artisan-frame. §9.2's artisan-frame is the primary success criterion A3 must pass; A3 is what gets specified.
- A3 is **not** A1. A1 (Hall of Fame & Shame) surfaces the championship-week row per season (the `championship_roll.md` view) plus worst-seasons and blowouts-hall content. A3 surfaces the *full bracket* — the preliminary and semifinal rounds A1's championship-roll column does not cover — plus cross-era playoff records and the bridesmaid leaderboard. **A3 and A1 share the `compute_championship_roll` substrate primitive** (Step 1 §4.4 empirically confirmed); the surfaces are presentation-disjoint per the selection-prep §3.1 absorption-finding boundary. A3 cross-links to A1's championship-roll row rather than re-rendering it.
- A3 is **not** A2. A2 (Draft History Vault) is auction-derived (`DRAFT_PICK` + `WEEKLY_PLAYER_SCORE` + `player_directory`); A3 is playoff-derived (`WEEKLY_MATCHUP_RESULT` via the playoff-detection trick). The substrates are disjoint.
- A3 is **not** a real-time bracket tracker. A3 is a retrospective archive. The active-bracket consumption — following the playoffs week-by-week as they happen — is the weekly recap's territory (E1). A3's bracket presentation updates once per year at end-of-NFL-season, not incrementally per playoff round (per §8 D4.2-Beta and the D4.2-Gamma exclusion at selection-prep §8.2).
- A3 is **not** the Surface Admission Test. Per A1 Reading 1 inheritance, A3 ships as a single Phase 11 surface; the Surface Admission Test's predecessor-state (Roadmap §5.1) is **not advanced** by A3's registration (four specs now exist post-A3; the one-content-class-admission-attempted requirement still gating).

### 3.5 Notable moments triggering push distribution (lands gap from Step 2 §4.3)

**Expected push cadence:** at most 1-2 events per NFL season per A3's standalone triggers, and **most NFL seasons will trigger zero A3 pushes** — the cross-era records are stable; the Per-Season Playoff Bracket sub-shape is a structural surface that does not generate "record falls" events (a new bracket is added every season, but adding a bracket is a revision-point update, not a push trigger). ### 3.6 Surface framing copy and W17/W18 collapse-by-content (lands gaps #1 and #5 from Step 2 §8.3)

**Scope-declaration line.** A3's surface displays a scope-declaration line at the header level. Recommended phrasing (subject to refinement at implementation; Voice Profile §6 territory):

Alternatives the implementer may elect at rendering time (without re-litigating the framing call):

**W17/W18 collapse-by-content (lands gap #1).** Per Step 1 §4.3 (D2-γ): the post-2021 seasons (2021-2025) each contain duplicate W17/W18 championship rows in the `WEEKLY_MATCHUP_RESULT` substrate, with verbatim-identical winner, loser, and scores. A3's Per-Season Playoff Bracket sub-shape adopts **Option 1 — collapse-by-content at the derivation layer**: when two consecutive playoff weeks have identical (`winner_id`, `loser_id`, `winner_score`, `loser_score`) tuples, the derivation treats the later week as a duplicate and emits only the earlier week as the "Championship" round. This is a fully-deterministic derivation rule with no platform-knowledge dependency.

Per Step 2 §6.1 / §6.5: Option 1 was the anchor recommendation for two reasons — it is the §2.3 silence-over-speculation default (the substrate has redundancy; the derivation collapses redundancy cleanly without speculating about cause), AND it makes the cross-era bracket-position semantics coherent (under collapse-by-content, both eras present as a clean 3-round bracket: preliminary → semifinal → championship; the cross-era leaderboards in §3.1's Cross-Season Playoff Records pool 3-round-pre-2021 and 3-round-post-2021 seasons with stable bracket-position semantics).

**Bracket-shape framing copy (lands gap #5).** Under W17/W18 collapse-by-content, both eras present as a clean 3-round bracket. The framing copy notes the week-label shift across the 2021 NFL format-shift (pre-2021: preliminary W14 / semifinal W15 / championship W16; post-2021: preliminary W15 / semifinal W16 / championship W17, with the substrate's W18 duplicate collapsed). This is analog to A1 spec §3.6's digital-era declaration and §3.7's format-shift normalization — A3's version is elevated to bracket-structure scope because A3 consumes the entire bracket, not just the championship-week subset A1 consumed.

**What A3 does NOT do on W17/W18:** A3 does not investigate the substrate-origin of the duplication. ### 3.7 Bridesmaids — runner-up leg only; almost leg dropped from v1 (lands gap #4 from Step 2 §8.3)

The Bridesmaids sub-shape per §3.1 ships the **championship-runner-up leg only.** The "one-game-out-of-playoffs" almost leg (D50 `detect_the_almost`) is **dropped from v1.**

**Reasoning** (per Step 1 §3.3 / §6.2):

- The runner-up leg is **dense and narratively complete on its own.** Step 1 §3.3 found 8 distinct franchises appearing as championship runner-up across the 16 digital-era seasons; franchises 0002 / 0005 / 0008 each have 3 runner-up finishes, with 0008 holding 3 runner-ups and **zero** championships — the perennial-bridesmaid archetype, the densest "close-but-no-cigar" story in PFL Buddies' substrate. The runner-up leg carries the §9.2 artisan-frame weight (Voice Profile §5's affectionate-memory framing) without the almost leg.
- The almost leg is **substrate-thin.** Step 1 §3.3 found D50's production threshold (`min_times=3`) produces **zero** PFL Buddies angles; the full leaderboard at `min_times=1` surfaces only 4 franchises, with a maximum count of 2. Shipping the almost leg at v1 would require lowering D50's threshold to 1 (producing a 4-row table) — a thin presentation relative to the dense runner-up leg.
- Per Step 1 §6.2: both choices (lower the threshold vs drop the leg) were defensible; the runner-up leg is the substantively-richer leg. The spec elects the drop: the Bridesmaids sub-shape is the runner-up leg, and it is narratively complete.

**The almost leg is preserved as a future-expansion path.** If at a future revision-point the founder elects to admit the almost leg — at a lowered threshold, or once the substrate has accumulated more seasons — it is a §7 governance decision, not a silent expansion. The drop-from-v1 is a scope decision, not a permanent exclusion.

**A side-finding recorded for cross-cluster carry-forward** (per Step 1 §6.2): D50's production-side default fires only at `min_times=3` in real recap context, which means D50 has been **silent across PFL Buddies' entire production history** to date. This is a separate finding — D50 may be a candidate for either a calibration revisit (Phase 10 / Tier 5 observation territory) or an admission that the detector is unfit-for-purpose for this league's playoff-field density. **Out-of-scope for A3; recorded here for the cross-cluster carry-forward and the Roadmap revision sweep.**

### 3.8 Cross-Season Playoff Records — per-season semantics and streak inclusion (lands gaps #2 and #3 from Step 2 §8.3)

**Per-season set semantics (lands gap #2).** A3's Cross-Season Playoff Records sub-shape lifts D39's (`detect_championship_history`) internal cross-season aggregation. Per Step 1 §6.3: D39 builds an internal `playoff_appearances: dict[str, int]` that increments **per-matchup**, not per-season — a franchise playing in 2 playoff weeks of a single season gets `+= 2`. The over-counting is silent in D39's production behavior (D39's surfaced angles read only `champ_appearances`, never the over-counted `playoff_appearances`; the internal dict is computed-but-unused).

A3's lift **must use per-season set semantics**: a franchise's playoff-appearance count is the number of *seasons* it appeared in the playoffs, incremented once per franchise per season. The implementation **does not directly consume D39's internal `playoff_appearances` dict**; it builds the count with a per-season `set[str]` of playoff participants. This invariant is specified explicitly (and re-stated at §6.x below) so a future maintainer does not "fix" the count by importing D39's internal logic and inheriting the over-counting.

**Streak inclusion (lands gap #3).** Both the longest-playoff-active streak and the longest-playoff-drought streak are **elected into v1.** Per Step 1 §6.4: the dense playoff field (every franchise made the playoffs ≥11 of 16 seasons; the longest drought across all franchises is 2 seasons) dampens streak-detection narrative drama — "franchise 0006's 4-year active streak" is true but carries less narrative weight than a streak in a league where droughts of 5-10 years are common. Step 2 named three options (surface both dimensions; surface only the active dimension; drop streaks from v1). The spec elects **surface both dimensions** for three reasons:

- Both dimensions are mathematically clean (Step 1 §3.2 computed them without ambiguity).
- The dense-field fact is itself part of the league's identity — a playoff field where every franchise has made the playoffs ≥11 of 16 seasons and no franchise has ever missed 3 in a row is a *substrate fact* that the cross-era leaderboard honestly depicts. Suppressing the drought dimension because the droughts are short would be editorializing against the substrate.
- The §9.2 artisan-frame is served by the honest depiction: franchise 0002's 12-season active streak is a real dynastic-frequency story, and the symmetry of showing both the active and drought dimensions is more substrate-honest than showing only the flattering one.

**Per-position-records-style analog:** A3's Cross-Season Playoff Records sub-shape is A3's analog of A2's §3.8 per-position-records subsection — a sub-shape with multiple internal dimensions (appearance counts + championship-matchup counts + active streaks + drought streaks). All four dimensions ship at v1; the streak dimensions are not gated.

## 6. Surface-specific invariants

A3's invariants beyond the substrate's invariants:

### 6.1 Archive content is substrate-derivable per D3-Alpha

Every displayed value in A3's archive traces to a canonical `WEEKLY_MATCHUP_RESULT` event via the playoff-detection trick and the `compute_championship_roll` / D39 / runner-up-leg aggregations at §3.1. The implementer **does not introduce commissioner-curated labels, narrative annotations, or non-substrate-derivable claims** at v1.

This invariant binds the implementation regardless of editorial impulse: if a row "needs" a label to read well, the implementation either (a) elects the descriptive substrate-derived rendering, (b) defers the row, or (c) surfaces the row without the label.

### 6.2 W17/W18 collapse-by-content is a deterministic derivation rule

Per §3.6: the post-2021 W17/W18 duplicate championship rows are collapsed by content at the derivation layer — when two consecutive playoff weeks have identical (`winner_id`, `loser_id`, `winner_score`, `loser_score`) tuples, the later week is treated as a duplicate and only the earlier week is emitted as the "Championship" round.

This invariant binds: the collapse rule is **content-based, not era-based.** The implementation does not hardcode "for post-2021 seasons, suppress W18" — it applies the content-equality test. If the substrate later changes (re-ingestion removes the duplication; or a genuine two-week championship structure with different scores appears), the content-based rule continues to produce the correct result without amendment. A3 does not investigate or speculate about the duplication's substrate-origin (§3.6); cause-investigation is out-of-scope and belongs to the adapter test surface or a Phase 12 ingest-audit.

### 6.3 Cross-Season Playoff Records use per-season set semantics

Per §3.8: A3's playoff-appearance counts are per-season (a franchise appearing in N playoff weeks of one season counts as 1 playoff-season appearance, not N). The implementation **does not directly consume D39's internal `playoff_appearances` dict** (which increments per-matchup); it builds the count with a per-season `set[str]` of playoff participants.

This invariant binds against a specific future-maintainer hazard: D39's internal per-matchup over-counting is silent in D39's own production behavior, so a maintainer "lifting D39's logic" could inherit the over-counting without noticing. The spec states the per-season semantics explicitly so the implementation and any future revision are bound to it.

### 6.4 Archive layout invariants

- `index.md` is the entry point and contains the scope-declaration header.

- Archive updates are append-only at the git level: new versions overwrite the file content; the prior version is preserved in git history.

The layout is enforced by a layout-invariant test (analog to A1's `Tests/test_hall_of_fame_archive_layout_v1.py`) at implementation.
