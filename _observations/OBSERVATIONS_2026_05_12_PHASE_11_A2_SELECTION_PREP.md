### 3.1 The substrate-window scope finding

The Roadmap §2.2 framing — *"across 17 seasons of digital history"* — carries **two layered drifts** that the selection-prep characterizes here and registers as a finding for a future Roadmap revision sweep (§10 below):

1. **17 → 16 digital era seasons.** Same drift A1 Step 1 §4.2 surfaced for A1, and A1 spec §3.1 corrected to "the 16-season digital window (2010-2025)." The pre-2010 league memory is real but not substrate-derivable (the league has been continuous since the mid-1980s per Voice Profile §1; the digital substrate begins at 2010 with the first MFL season).

2. **16 → 8 auction-era seasons.** This is **new for A2**, not present for A1. The auction-draft substrate file declares its own scope at the docstring level: *"PFL Buddies context: $200 auction budget, 17 roster spots, data from 2018-2025."* (auction_draft_angles_v1.py:10). **The auction format substrate covers 8 seasons (2018-2025); the pre-2018 portion of the digital era (2010-2017) is either pre-auction-format (snake draft) or unaggregated against the auction-detector suite.** D2 below probes the actual canonical-event ingest coverage; the docstring's framing claim is taken as a *structural-read prior*, not as substrate-confirmed truth.

**Both drifts compound for A2.** Roadmap §2.2 says "17 seasons of digital history"; the substrate-derivable A2 archive is most likely 8 seasons (2018-2025). The eight-vs-seventeen-seasons compounded drift is a substantial scope distortion. A2's identity in this memo is therefore declared at:

> **A2 — Draft History Vault.** A browseable archive of PFL Buddies' auction-draft history across the auction era (2018-2025; 8 seasons per substrate-readiness prior, pending D2 verification). The archive surfaces auction prices, position-cost trends, and value-vs-cost outcomes derived from canonical `DRAFT_PICK` events (with `bid_amount`) joined to `WEEKLY_PLAYER_SCORE` production data and `player_directory` position metadata. Content is substrate-derivable per the D3-Alpha disposition inherited from A1 (see §6.3 below).

This scope declaration is the A2 analog of A1 spec §3.1's "16-season digital window" framing. A2's framing copy at the spec session will declare the auction era boundary explicitly at the header level (the A1 spec §3.6 framing-copy precedent applies).

### 3.2 What A2 is

A2 is the Phase 11 admissible surface that **presents the substrate's auction-draft data as a browseable record of league auction memory across the auction-format era.** The surface's content is composed of substrate-derived facts (auction picks with bid amounts; aggregated points-per-dollar; per-position price trends; per-season most-expensive picks) with at most light connective prose; the narrative footprint is small by comparison with the weekly recap (whose narrative arc carries the weekly-cycle storytelling load).

## 4. Candidate sub-shape enumeration

### 4.1 Method

For each candidate sub-shape, this memo addresses:

- **Substrate-coverage** — what canonical event type(s) the sub-shape consumes; which D20-D28 detector(s) already do the aggregation work the sub-shape would consume; what cross-season scope the detector currently operates over (single-season vs already multi-season); whether the substrate-readiness prior is "favorable" (existing implementation directly addresses the sub-shape) or "favorable-with-extension" (existing implementation addresses the within-season pattern but cross-season aggregation needs new code).
- **Overlap with A1 / A3** — where the sub-shape's content could equivalently belong to A1 or A3. Per §4.3 below, A2 is substantively disjoint from both (a cleaner overlap landscape than A1's was).
- **Initial product-criteria fit** — light gradient pass against demonstrates-what-SquadVault-IS, Sunday/Monday-night-use, implementation-risk, learning-value, §9.2 artisan-frame fit. This is a gradient read, not a screening test; decision-readiness Step 2 does the actual comparison.

The substrate-coverage characterization is anchored on the Architectural Audit Section 3 D-detector inventory (D20-D28 family) and on the structural read of `src/squadvault/core/recaps/context/auction_draft_angles_v1.py` performed for this memo. Per Anti-Drift §10 #5 (silence over speculation on draft-event substrate-coverage specifics), the structural read characterizes detector scope and data dependencies; it does **not** run SQL probes against `canonical_events` to confirm auction-event ingestion completeness — that is Step 1's job.

### 8.1 The cadence shape

- **Annually at end-of-season (~February)** — when full-season `WEEKLY_PLAYER_SCORE` data resolves; bust and bargain leaderboards recompute against the new production data; new top-1 entries may displace standing records.
- **At notable in-season moments** — rare but possible (a mid-season trade for an auction-acquired player that dramatically reframes the pick's bust/bargain status, though A2's bust/bargain detectors don't currently surface this real-time).
