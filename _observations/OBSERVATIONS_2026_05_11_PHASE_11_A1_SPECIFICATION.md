## 3. Identity and scope

### 3.1 What A1 is

**A1 — Hall of Fame & Shame** is a registered Phase 11 product surface presenting a browseable archive of PFL Buddies' digital-era league records. The archive surfaces three sub-shapes at v1, each rendered as a per-leaderboard view against canonical events from the substrate's append-only ledger:

- **Championship Roll** — per-season list of league champions across the 16-season digital window (2010-2025), with championship-week record and PF/PA for the championship matchup.
- **Worst-Season Tracking** — top-N season-records by inverse-quality (lowest win count, lowest PF, with the 2021 format-shift acknowledged at display per §3.7).
- **Blowouts Hall** — top-N single-week matchup-margins across the digital window, with winner/loser franchises, season, week, scores, and margin.

A1's content is **substrate-derivable per Step 2's D3-Alpha disposition.** Every value displayed traces to a canonical event (`WEEKLY_MATCHUP_RESULT` for all three sub-shapes per Step 1 §3) or to an aggregation primitive in `src/squadvault/core/recaps/data/league_history_v1.py` / `franchise_deep_angles_v1.py`. No commissioner-curated labels; no narrative annotation; the substrate facts stand.

A1's **declared temporal scope is the digital era: 2010-2025 (16 seasons).** The league predates the substrate by ~27 years (mid-1980s inception per Voice Profile §1; selection-prep / Step 1 use "1983" as the precise inception year). The pre-2010 league memory is GAF-shaped by definition (real, culturally-significant, not substrate-derivable) and is acknowledged at copy level per §3.6 framing but is **out of scope for A1's v1** per the D3-Alpha + Reading 1 joint disposition.

### 3.2 What A1 is not

- A1 is **not** the substrate. The substrate (canonical events, `franchise_directory`, aggregation primitives, detector suite) produces the per-(season, week, franchise) facts; A1 reads those facts and presents them as cross-season leaderboards.
- A1 is **not** a meta-surface. Per the Reading 1 election, A1 ships as one surface with one bounded content scope. A2 (Draft History Vault) and A3 (Championship Timeline) are admissible Cluster A successors with their own selection chains, not A1 content classes.
- A1 is **not** an editorial layer. The narrative voice of weekly recaps lives in E1's content (per E1 spec §6.3). A1 presents records, not narratives. Surface-level copy is minimal and descriptive per §3.6.
- A1 is **not** an engagement surface. A1 does not track read-rates, page-views, browse durations, or any analogous metric. Per Reset Memo §2.3 fifth principle and Operational Plan §10 commitment #4: **no analytics, optimization, engagement loops, or prediction — ever.**
- A1 is **not** a social network. Per Reset Memo §4.4 (social-surface vs. social-network distinction; parent §4 Cluster A pass): no follower graph, no comment threads, no opt-in subscription, no member discoverability mechanisms, no recommendation. The archive is a self-contained presentation; league members find it and read it.
- A1 is **not** the artisan-frame. Per E1 spec §3 (carried forward): the artisan-frame is the §9.2 primary success criterion that A1 must pass. A1 is what gets specified; the artisan-frame is a test A1 must satisfy.

### 3.5 Notable moments triggering push distribution (lands gap #5 from Step 2 §8.3)

**Notable moments** for D4.1-Gamma push distribution are bounded to **three triggers**, each tied to a clear "top of league history" signal:

1. **A new championship is crowned.** Once per NFL season, ~February, when the Phony Bowl is decided. Push distribution event references the new champion, season, and the championship matchup score.
2. **A new top-1 entry in Worst-Season Tracking.** At most once per NFL season, when end-of-season aggregation lands a new worst-record holder. Push distribution event references the franchise, season, record, and PF.
3. **A new top-1 entry in Blowouts Hall.** At most once per NFL season, when a single-week matchup-margin exceeds the standing top-1. Push distribution event references winner, loser, season/week, scores, and margin.

**Explicitly NOT triggers:**

- Top-3 / top-5 / top-10 entries (too noisy; would generate a push for every leaderboard change).
- Cross-tabulation changes or franchise-specific updates within sub-shapes.
- Browse-activity signals (engagement-loop-adjacent; forbidden per §2.3 and §4.4).

**Expected push cadence:** at most 3 events per NFL season, all clustered around end-of-season. Most NFL seasons will not trigger all three (the top-1 thresholds in each leaderboard are stable across most seasons; Step 1 §6.5 noted 2025 was unusual in producing simultaneous top-1 updates in worst-season and blowouts).

**Confidence on this disposition: medium-high.** The three triggers are well-scoped to honor Voice Profile §5 "Piling On" pattern without leaking into engagement-loop territory. The founder may amend at the next revision-point if the cadence proves to be too sparse (notable moments missed) or too noisy (push events that don't resonate).

### 3.7 Format-shift normalization at display (lands gap #3 from Step 2 §8.3)

The 2021 format-shift (Step 1 §6.2; §4.3) means absolute-count comparisons across the era boundary are not strictly comparable. The 14-regular-season-games-per-franchise floor pre-2021 vs. 15 post-2021 makes 1-13 structurally impossible pre-2021 (max 13 regular-season losses) and possible 2021-onward.

**A1's normalization disposition:**

- **Worst-Season Tracking:** display absolute records (W-L-T) as the primary column, with a footnote or column-header note acknowledging the format shift ("14 regular-season games per franchise 2010-2020; 15 games 2021+"). **Do not normalize away the era distinction** — let the substrate facts stand. Optionally include winning-percentage (W / (W+L+T)) as a secondary column for era-spanning comparability; per-game PF (PF / total-games) likewise as a secondary column. The display invites the reader to make era-aware comparisons; the spec does not impose them.
- **Blowouts Hall:** no normalization needed. Margin is era-stable (it's a per-matchup quantity; not aggregate). Display absolute margins.
- **Championship Roll:** no normalization needed. Champion-per-season is era-stable.

**Era-sectioning** (separate pre-2021 and post-2021 tables) is **not elected** at v1 — it would split the leaderboards into two short windows (11 seasons + 5 seasons) and lose the cross-era continuity the league experiences. Single-table-with-footnote preserves continuity while declaring the era distinction.

**Confidence on normalization disposition: medium-high.** Standard practice for archive presentations across format changes; matches Step 1 §6.2's "spec-stage disposition, multiple defensible options" framing.

### 4.2 §4.4 — Tone Engine boundary and social-surface vs. social-network

**Tone Engine boundary.** A1 does not modify, extend, or apply post-hoc adjustment to the Tone Engine. A1's surface copy is minimal (the §3.6 framing declaration; per-leaderboard column headers; per-row descriptive presentation). Where surface copy emerges, it is descriptive metadata (e.g., "Championship Roll — Digital Era — 16 Seasons") rather than tone-iterated narrative. The Tone Engine governs E1's recap-artifact content; A1 inherits no tone-engine load.

**Social-surface vs. social-network.** A1 is a social *surface* — league members view shared league history through a self-contained archive. A1 is not a social *network*:

- No follower graph, no member-to-member subscription, no opt-in mechanism that creates network effects.
- No comment threads, no member-write-rights, no member-side annotation.
- No public discoverability mechanism; A1's archive is discoverable through commissioner-mediated push distribution events (§3.5) and through the archive's own URL/path, which the league knows.
- No recommendation engine, no member-segmentation.
- The archive is a thing-in-itself; league members find it and read it. The social fabric exists in the league; A1 presents content for that fabric, not a fabric of its own.

The push distribution events at notable moments (D4.1-Gamma) are **not** an engagement-loop. They are operational signaling that the archive has updated at a top-of-league-history threshold — analog to E1's distribution events at the recap-artifact level. The bounded-to-three-triggers scope (§3.5) prevents push-cadence drift into "we're showing you this every week" territory which would be engagement-loop-adjacent.

**Confidence: high.** Both tests verifiable from the specified shape (no member-write-rights; bounded push; absent recommendation/segmentation/network mechanisms).

## 6. Surface-specific invariants

A1's invariants beyond the substrate's invariants:

### 6.1 Archive content is substrate-derivable per D3-Alpha

Every displayed value in A1's archive traces to a canonical event via the named aggregation primitives. The implementer **does not introduce commissioner-curated labels, narrative annotations, or non-substrate-derivable claims** at v1.

If implementation finds that the substrate later extends backward (e.g., a pre-2010 MFL backfill becomes available), the declared scope updates with the substrate at the next revision-point — not silently.

### 6.3 Push distribution at notable moments is bounded to three triggers

Per §3.5: a new championship; a new top-1 in Worst-Season Tracking; a new top-1 in Blowouts Hall. **Not** top-N entries for N > 1; **not** cross-tabulation changes; **not** browse-activity signals; **not** any signal that could leak into engagement-loop territory.

This invariant binds the push-mechanism implementation regardless of operational impulse: if the founder later elects to add a fourth trigger, the addition is a revision-point decision per §8, not a silent expansion.

### 6.4 Archive layout invariants

The archive at `archive/hall_of_fame_and_shame/` (path subject to implementation refinement) follows a stable layout:

- `index.md` is the entry point and contains the scope-declaration header.
- Per-sub-shape markdown files (`championship_roll.md`, `worst_seasons.md`, `blowouts_hall.md` or similar) are siblings of `index.md`.
- Archive updates are append-only at the git level: new versions overwrite the file content; the prior version is preserved in git history.

The layout is enforced by a layout-invariant test (analog to E1's `Tests/test_archive_layout_v1.py`) at implementation.

### 6.5 Append-only at every surface

- Canonical events: read-only at A1's boundary. A1 does not write back.
- Archive markdown: regenerations overwrite file content but git history preserves all prior versions. Conceptually append-only at the version layer.
- Push distribution events: recorded in the group-text thread's existing infrastructure (the league's own append-only conversation log) plus optionally in an A1-side push-event archive (if specified at implementation; not required at v1).

### 6.6 No engagement instrumentation

Forbidden operations (binding per Reset Memo §2.3 fifth principle + §4.4 + Operational Plan §10 commitment #4):

- Read-tracking (per-member, per-row, per-leaderboard, per-session).
- Browse-duration capture.
- Click-tracking, referrer-tracking, source-tracking.
- Recommendation engines (e.g., "based on what you browsed, you may also like...").
- Audience segmentation (e.g., "newer members see this; veterans see that").
- A/B testing of layouts, copy, or content.
- Scheduling optimization (push timing, push frequency).
- Growth-loop construction (e.g., share-this; invite-a-friend; engagement-leaderboards).

This invariant is preserved by **what A1 is forbidden to do**, not by what A1 is instructed to do. The invariant is the doctrinal-fit guarantee per §2.3 fifth principle.

### 6.7 Specification governs implementation; implementation does not silently revise specification

Per E1 spec §6.7 precedent: the specification's invariants bind the implementation. If implementation work introduces a behavior that conflicts with the spec, either (a) the implementation conforms or (b) the spec amends explicitly at a revision-point. Silent divergence between spec and implementation is a finding; it does not resolve by either side defaulting.

**Confidence on invariants: high.** Each invariant is grounded in the Step 2 dispositions or in inherited doctrinal substance.
