Ask-PFL ("Ask the Historian") — Constitutional Adjudication
Date: 2026-07-06. Status: governing ruling, founder-ratified, append-only. Scope: rules the permitted FORM of any natural-language query surface over the canonical database, BEFORE any brief is authored. No brief may issue except in conformance with this ruling.
The tension
A query surface conflicts with four frozen principles at once: silence-over-speculation (an open box invites unanswerable questions), no-prediction (it invites forecasts), no-engagement-loops (chat interfaces are structurally habit-forming), and — decisively — "AI assists; humans approve publication": interactive answers are generated without the human approval gate that governs every published narrative. Any form of Ask-PFL must resolve all four, not style around them.
The three forms, ruled
Form A — deterministic retrieval with templated rendering: PERMITTED. The natural-language input is parsed to a bounded catalog of question intents (head-to-head records, award holders, season results, streaks, custody history — each backed by an existing shipped derivation). The answer is assembled from canonical facts using pre-rendered strings (the score-string playbook: render at the data layer, verify verbatim). No free generative prose reaches the member. A question outside the catalog, or whose answer is not in the ledger, receives a dignified refusal ("The record doesn't hold that."), never a guess. This is constitutionally clean because nothing is generated: it is search wearing natural language, every answer is a derived presentation of verified facts, and silence is the default failure mode.
Form B — live LLM-composed answers (recap-style prose, verifier-gated): BLOCKED absent constitutional amendment. Even with the full verifier pipeline, interactive answers ship to a member with no human approval step. The recap pipeline is constitutional because a human approves before publication; Form B removes that gate. Defining interactive answers as "not publication" would be an amendment to a frozen principle — a founder constitutional act, recorded append-only, not an implementation choice. Until and unless that amendment is made deliberately, Form B does not ship.
Form C — open-ended chat over the database: FORBIDDEN. Unbounded scope guarantees speculation, invites prediction, and is an engagement surface by construction. Not buildable under the frozen principles in any configuration.
Form A guardrails (bind any future brief)

Bounded intent catalog — every answerable question maps to a named, shipped derivation; the catalog is enumerable and tested. No open-ended composition.
Retrospective only — prediction-shaped questions are refused by class, with copy that names the principle ("SquadVault keeps the record; it never forecasts.").
Silence over speculation, literally — no partial answers, no "best guesses," no fuzzy matches presented as facts. Out-of-catalog → the dignified refusal.
Provenance on every answer — each response carries its receipt (fact IDs, the two-tier provenance treatment), consistent with the dual-layer model: the answer is a lens on the ledger and shows its work.
No engagement mechanics — no suggested follow-ups, no "you might also ask," no query history feed, no notifications, no tracking of usage. One question, one answer, done. The surface must not court return visits; the record's value does that.
No new facts — answers render existing derivations; if a question requires a derivation that doesn't exist, the answer is the refusal, and the missing derivation is (at most) registered as engine work through normal governance.

Sequencing (unchanged)
Completion Plan v1.4 sequences Ask-PFL post-launch (Phase 12), and this ruling does not pull it forward. Reasons of substance, not caution: (a) the intent catalog is far richer once a live season's data flows and members have onboarded; (b) pre-season capacity is committed to the reveal surfaces and the pre-season baseline measurement; (c) Form A's value depends on the shipped derivations it fronts — every derivation shipped between now and then widens the catalog for free. When its window arrives, the brief is authored against this ruling.
Disposition
Form A permitted under guardrails 1–6 · Form B blocked pending a deliberate constitutional amendment · Form C forbidden · sequencing per v1.4. Any future Ask-PFL brief cites this memo and conforms; deviations are escalated, not improvised.
