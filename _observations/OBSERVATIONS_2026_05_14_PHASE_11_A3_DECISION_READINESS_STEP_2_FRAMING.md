### 4.2 D4.2 — cadence anchor (the load-bearing call)

Three candidate anchors per selection-prep §6.4 / §8.2:

- **D4.2-Alpha** — NFL Week 1 (~2026-09-08), shared with E1, A1, A2. Cross-surface consistency.
- **D4.2-Beta** — End-of-NFL-season (~February 2027), when the new championship and bracket land. A3's substance-complete moment, with cross-surface A1-coherence.
- **D4.2-Gamma** — Active playoff weeks (~December-January). Excluded at selection-prep §8.2 on cadence-noise grounds (the bracket is incomplete during active playoff weeks; multi-revision in-season cadence has no analog in E1/A1/A2 rhythms).

The election is between Alpha and Beta. This is the first Phase 11 surface where the chat-session recommendation diverges from the D4.2-Alpha inheritance default.

#### Why A1 and A2 elected Alpha

A1 elected D4.2-Alpha (A1 Step 2 §4.6) and A2 elected D4.2-Alpha (A2 Step 2 §4.2) on three considerations: cross-surface consistency (one calendar moment for all Phase 11 revisions); stability under substrate uncertainty (for A2, the unresolved 2021 gap); and niche-generality (NFL Week 1 is a generally-applicable anchor; A2's auction-night alternative was PFL-calendar-specific).

For A2 specifically, the D4.2-Gamma (auction-night) substance-honesty argument was *real but outweighed* — A2 Step 2 §4.2 recorded it as "the genuinely-defensible alternative" but landed on Alpha because auction-night "is a *new substrate-accumulation* moment with no analog in A1's or E1's rhythms," so the cross-surface-consistency argument dominated.

#### Why A3's Beta argument is materially stronger than A2's Gamma was

The selection-prep §6.4 / §8.2 named this explicitly, and Step 1's findings reinforce it. Three considerations distinguish A3's D4.2-Beta from A2's D4.2-Gamma:

1. **End-of-NFL-season has cross-surface coherence with A1; auction-night did not.** A2's auction-night moment had no analog in A1's revision rhythm — that is precisely why A2's cross-surface-consistency argument pointed to Alpha. A3's end-of-NFL-season moment **is A1's revision moment**: A1's championship-roll new-row falls at end-of-NFL-season when the new champion is crowned (A1 Step 1 §6.5 noted 2025 produced simultaneous top-1 updates clustered around end-of-season). A3's bracket archive and cross-era leaderboards update at the *same event* — the championship landing. **Under D4.2-Beta, A3 and A1 share a natural revision rhythm.** The cross-surface-consistency argument, which pointed to Alpha for A2, points to *Beta* for A3 when "cross-surface" is read as A1-coherence rather than E1-coherence.

2. **Step 1 §4.4 confirmed A3 and A1 share the `compute_championship_roll` substrate primitive.** The substrate-sharing is now empirically confirmed, not just structural-read. When the end-of-2026-NFL-season championship lands, *both* A1's championship roll and A3's bracket archive consume the same new `WEEKLY_MATCHUP_RESULT` events through the same primitive. Anchoring A3's revision-point on a *different* calendar moment than A1's would mean the two surfaces that share a substrate primitive revise on different schedules — an operational incoherence that D4.2-Beta avoids.

3. **A3's substance-completeness moment IS end-of-NFL-season, unambiguously.** Step 1 §8.1 (carried from selection-prep) established A3's per-year update rhythm is single-phase: the bracket completes and the cross-era leaderboards potentially reorder at exactly one moment per year — when the championship lands. A2's was two-phase (auction night + end-of-season), which created genuine ambiguity about which phase to anchor on; A1's was single-phase end-of-season but A1 elected Alpha for E1-cross-surface-consistency at a time when A1 was the *first* cluster-A surface and E1-coherence was the dominant available consideration. A3 is the *third* cluster-A surface; the cross-surface landscape has changed (see consideration 4).

4. **The cross-surface landscape has shifted since A1's election.** When A1 elected Alpha, E1-coherence was the dominant cross-surface consideration (E1 was the only other Phase 11 surface). A2 then inherited Alpha. But A3 selection-prep §8.2 made a sharp observation: "A1's actual update rhythm per its spec is end-of-season-anchored" — A1 *elected* the Alpha calendar-anchor, but A1's substantive content-update *happens* at end-of-season. The same is true for A2's end-of-season bust/bargain leaderboard updates. **The Phase 11 surfaces' substantive content largely updates at end-of-NFL-season regardless of their elected calendar-anchor.** A3 electing D4.2-Beta makes A3's *elected anchor* match its *substantive update rhythm* — and matches the substantive update rhythm of A1 and A2 as well, even though A1 and A2 elected the Alpha calendar-anchor.

#### The counter-consideration for Alpha

The cross-surface-consistency argument for Alpha is not nothing. Under D4.2-Beta:

- A3's elected anchor differs from E1's, A1's, and A2's *elected* anchors (all Alpha). The "one calendar moment for all Phase 11 revisions" property is broken at the elected-anchor level — though, per consideration 4, it was already substantively broken at the actual-update-rhythm level.
- A3's spec-revision discipline runs on a different calendar reminder than the other three surfaces. This is a real operational cost: the founder must track two revision-calendar moments (NFL Week 1 for E1/A1/A2; end-of-NFL-season for A3) rather than one.
- The spec session lands ~9-12 months before A3's first D4.2-Beta revision-point fires; under D4.2-Alpha the first revision-point would fire ~3-4 months after the spec session. The longer gap-to-first-revision is a (mild) argument for Alpha on "validate the revision discipline sooner" grounds.

#### Disposition: **D4.2-Beta** (recommended; founder may elect Alpha)

A3's revision-point anchors on **end-of-NFL-season (~February 2027)**, when the new championship and full playoff bracket land.

**Reasoning:**

1. End-of-NFL-season has cross-surface coherence *with A1* — the surface A3 shares a substrate primitive with — in a way auction-night did not have with A1 for A2. The cross-surface-consistency argument, properly read, points to Beta for A3.
2. A3's substance-completeness moment is unambiguously end-of-NFL-season (single-phase update rhythm; Step 1 §8.1).
3. A3 and A1 consume the same `compute_championship_roll` primitive on the same new events at end-of-NFL-season (Step 1 §4.4); anchoring them on the same revision moment is operationally coherent.
4. The Phase 11 surfaces' substantive content already updates at end-of-NFL-season regardless of elected anchor; D4.2-Beta makes A3's elected anchor honest to its actual rhythm.

**This is a divergence from A1/A2 precedent, and it is deliberate.** A3 selection-prep §6.4 and §8.2 both flagged that A3's Beta argument is "materially stronger than A2's Gamma was" and recommended surfacing D4.2 "as a genuine Step 2 call rather than a default-inheritance carry-forward." This memo does that, and the analysis lands on Beta. The recommendation is **not** that A1 and A2 should be revisited — their Alpha elections stand, and their substantive-content-updates-at-end-of-season behavior is compatible with an Alpha calendar-anchor. The recommendation is specific to A3: A3 is the surface where the substance-honesty pull and the cross-surface-coherence pull *both* point to Beta, which was not true for A1 (first surface; E1-coherence dominant) or A2 (auction-night had no A1-analog).

**The alternative the founder may elect (D4.2-Alpha):** anchored on NFL Week 1 for elected-anchor consistency with E1/A1/A2. If elected, A3's spec opens with a note that A3's substantive content-update (end-of-NFL-season) is calendar-separated from its revision-point-anchor (NFL Week 1) by ~6 months — the same calendar-separation A1 and A2 have under their Alpha elections. This is a coherent joint shape; it simply weights elected-anchor-consistency over substance-honesty-and-A1-coherence.

**Why the founder elects:** D4.2 is the load-bearing call this session surfaces, and it is the first Phase 11 D4.2 where the recommendation diverges from inheritance. The founder's product judgment under §9.2 adjudicates: the chat-session recommendation is Beta on the four-consideration reasoning above, but a founder who weights "one elected calendar moment for all Phase 11 surfaces" highly may legitimately elect Alpha.

**Confidence:** **Medium-high** on the recommendation. The four-consideration reasoning toward Beta is, in this memo's assessment, stronger than the cross-surface-consistency counter-argument for Alpha — but this is a genuine product-judgment call and the divergence-from-precedent raises the bar for founder confirmation. Higher confidence than A2's D4.2 (which was a closer call) because A3's substance-honesty and cross-surface-coherence arguments *align* rather than trade off.

### 4.3 D4.3 — push-distribution triggers

Scoped to the elected v1 sub-shape trio per A3 selection-prep §6.4 D4.3 and A1 spec §3.5 / A2 spec §3.5 precedent. The defining A3-specific feature: **A1 owns the new-championship push event** per the §3.1 cross-link discipline; A3 references rather than re-triggers. A3's standalone triggers are sparser than A1's or A2's because most cross-era playoff records are stable across seasons.

Three triggers, each tied to "top of league history" signals; bounded; explicitly NOT engagement-loop-adjacent:

1. **A new top-1 entry in cross-era playoff appearances (§4.2.2 dimension).** Fires at most once per NFL season at end-of-NFL-season when the completed playoff bracket resolves and a franchise's playoff-season count exceeds the standing top-1. Rare given the 16-season substrate and the stability of multi-appearance leaders (Step 1 §3.2: 0002 at 14 playoff seasons leads; a new top-1 requires a franchise to surpass that).
2. **A new top-1 entry in cross-era bridesmaid count (§4.2.4 runner-up leg).** Fires at most once per NFL season at end-of-NFL-season when a franchise's championship-runner-up count exceeds the standing top-1 (Step 1 §3.3: 0002 / 0005 / 0008 tied at 3 — a new top-1 requires a fourth runner-up finish by one of them, or a new entrant reaching 4).
3. **A new top-1 entry in longest-playoff-active streak (§4.2.2 streak dimension), if streaks are elected to v1.** Conditional on the §6.4 streak-inclusion adjudication. Fires at most once per NFL season at end-of-NFL-season. (Step 1 §3.2: 0002 holds a 12-season active streak — a new top-1 requires surpassing 12, which is structurally near-impossible in a 16-season substrate, so this trigger is near-dormant.)

**The new-championship push is NOT an A3 trigger** — it is A1's trigger per A1 spec §3.5, and A3's §3.1 cross-link discipline means A3 references A1's championship-roll row rather than re-pushing the championship event. This is the operative cross-link-discipline boundary for D4.3.

**Expected push cadence:** at most 1-2 events per NFL season per A3's standalone triggers, and **most NFL seasons will trigger zero A3 pushes** — the cross-era records are stable; the bracket-presentation sub-shape (§4.2.1) is a structural surface that does not generate "record falls" events (a new bracket is added every season, but adding a bracket row is a revision-point update, not a push trigger). A3's push cadence is the sparsest of the cluster-A surfaces.

**Explicitly NOT triggers** (per A1 spec §3.5 / A2 spec §3.5 precedent):

- Per-bracket-fill-in pushes during active playoff weeks (would generate a push per playoff round; couples to the D4.2-Gamma rejected-for-cadence-noise framing).
- Per-franchise-specific bracket updates (too granular; engagement-loop-adjacent).
- Top-3 / top-5 / top-10 entries in any cross-era leaderboard (too noisy).
- The new-championship event itself (A1 owns it; §3.1 cross-link discipline).
- Browse-activity signals (forbidden per §2.3 / §4.4).

**D4.2 interaction:** Under the recommended **D4.2-Beta** election, all three A3 push triggers fire at end-of-NFL-season — the *same* calendar moment as the revision-point. A3's push events and A3's revision-point are calendar-aligned under Beta. This is a coherence the recommendation's reasoning (§4.2) reinforces: A3's entire operational rhythm collapses to one calendar moment per year. Under D4.2-Alpha, the three triggers still fire at end-of-NFL-season (the triggers are substrate-event-driven, not anchor-driven), but they would be calendar-separated from the NFL-Week-1 revision-point by ~6 months — the same calendar-separation A1 and A2 have.

**Confidence:** **High** on the three-trigger enumeration and the A1-owns-new-championship cross-link discipline; **high** on the sparse-cadence framing; **high** on the D4.2-Beta calendar-alignment observation.
