The memo's value-add to the chain: a candidate-set characterization for A1 grounded in actual substrate evidence, a registered diagnostic list for decision-readiness, and the surface-vs-meta-surface question surfaced before it tries to resolve itself silently inside the specification session.

## 4. Candidate sub-shape enumeration

### 4.1 Method

For each candidate sub-shape, this memo addresses:

- **Substrate-coverage** — what canonical event type(s) the sub-shape consumes; whether the substrate currently supplies the required data; whether any existing detector (D-id from `Narrative_Angles_v2_Definitive_Scope.md`) already does the aggregation work the sub-shape would consume.
- **Overlap with A2 / A3** — where the sub-shape's content could equivalently belong to A2 (Draft History Vault) or A3 (Championship Timeline). Overlap is noted; adjudication deferred.
- **Initial product-criteria fit** — light pass against the parent memo's five product criteria (demonstrates-what-SquadVault-IS, league-Sunday/Monday-night-use, implementation-risk, learning-value, §9.2 artisan-frame fit). This is a *gradient* read, not a screening test; the decision-readiness session does the actual comparison.

The substrate-coverage characterization is anchored on the Architectural Audit (2026-04-16, `ARCHITECTURAL_AUDIT_2026_04_16.md` Section 2 table-by-table assessment and Section 3 detector inventory) and on `Narrative_Angles_v2_Definitive_Scope.md` Dimension 9 (Franchise History & Identity, D36–D50).

### 4.2 Sub-shape catalog

#### 4.2.1 Championship roll

- **Description:** List of champions by year across all 17 seasons. Optionally: back-to-back champions, longest gaps between titles, never-won-it list (the inverse).
- **Substrate-coverage:** Champion is **derivable, not stored** as an explicit canonical event. The playoff-detection trick (used in three places in current code per Architectural Audit Section 7: counting matchups per week → fewer than N/2 matchups indicates playoff/elimination → final playoff week's winner is champion) reads `WEEKLY_MATCHUP_RESULT` and returns the championship matchup per season. D39 (CHAMPIONSHIP_HISTORY) already implements playoff and championship appearance counts across seasons; lifting D39's logic into a render path is the natural pattern.
- **Substrate readiness:** **High.** `WEEKLY_MATCHUP_RESULT` is present across all 17 seasons per the LEAGUE_HISTORY temporal-scoping work validated at `8082040`. D39 is already in the detector suite. No new ingest paths; no new detector classes; no new schema.
- **Overlap with A3:** **Substantial.** A3 (Championship Timeline) is named in Roadmap §2.2 as "playoff brackets and outcomes across the 17 seasons." Championship roll is a single-column extraction from A3's full bracket presentation. **Adjudication:** if A1 ships single-surface, championship roll is naturally a v1 A1 sub-shape and A3's full bracket presentation defers as a later within-cluster pick; if A1 ships meta-surface, championship roll is an A1 content class and A3 is folded as a content class. Either reading is defensible.
- **Product-criteria gradient:** Demonstrates-what-SquadVault-IS — **highest** (17 seasons of league memory, rendered). Sunday/Monday-night-use — **low-medium** (browse-cadenced; spikes after each championship lands). Implementation-risk — **lowest** (pure consumer; D39 already lifts the data). Learning-value — **low** (the league already knows; the surface confirms). §9.2 artisan-frame fit — **medium-high** (champions are central to league memory, but the "affectionately remembers mistakes" framing leans toward shame, not fame, as the §9.2 sweet-spot).

#### 4.2.2 Worst-season tracking

- **Description:** Last-place finishers by year; lowest-scoring seasons; longest losing streaks (within a season; across seasons). The "worst seasons" inverse of championship roll.
- **Substrate-coverage:** `WEEKLY_MATCHUP_RESULT` aggregated by season-end standing. The playoff-detection trick again — the franchise that did not advance to the playoff is on the worst-season ledger. D42 (LUCKY_RECORD), D50 (THE_ALMOST), the streak detectors, and the worst-season-record field in `derive_league_history_v1` all already do components of this work. The "Miller went 1-13" reference in Voice Profile §5 is exactly this shape — the canonical pattern. Per session-brief `session_brief_league_history_scoping_phase_1.md`, `best_season_record` is a field name in `LeagueHistoryContextV1`; the worst-season inverse is structurally identical.
- **Substrate readiness:** **High.** Same substrate as championship roll. No new foundations.
- **Overlap with A2 / A3:** Low. Worst-season tracking is squarely A1 territory; doesn't overlap with the draft data A2 covers or the playoff-bracket structure A3 names.
- **Product-criteria gradient:** Demonstrates-what-SquadVault-IS — **high** (the league's longitudinal record made browseable, including the parts the league laughs about). Sunday/Monday-night-use — **low-medium** (browse-cadenced). Implementation-risk — **lowest** (substrate already aggregates). Learning-value — **low**. §9.2 artisan-frame fit — **highest** (this is precisely the "affectionately remembers mistakes" pattern. "Miller went 1-13" *is* the shape).

#### 4.2.3 Blowouts hall

- **Description:** Single-week largest margins of victory/defeat across all 17 seasons. Optionally: weeks of total league-wide carnage; closest games (the inverse, "nail-biter hall").
- **Substrate-coverage:** `WEEKLY_MATCHUP_RESULT` with margin computed. The primordial BLOWOUT and NAIL_BITER detectors (per Architectural Audit Section 3, "operates on TeamRecord.current_streak"; margin vs. season average) already compute this for weekly recap context. Across-seasons aggregation is a straightforward extension.
- **Substrate readiness:** **High.** Same substrate; the primordial detectors already do the math at within-season scope; cross-season extension is computational, not architectural.
- **Overlap with A2 / A3:** Negligible.
- **Product-criteria gradient:** Demonstrates-what-SquadVault-IS — **high**. Sunday/Monday-night-use — **low-medium**. Implementation-risk — **lowest**. Learning-value — **low**. §9.2 artisan-frame fit — **high** (blowouts and nail-biters are league-chat-density events; Voice Profile §5 cites "Blowouts get piled on. Close games get real-time commentary").

#### 4.2.4 Manager records (head-to-head)

- **Description:** Head-to-head records across all 17 seasons; longest rivalries; perennial cellar-dwellers; comeback seasons. Cross-tabulated by franchise pair.
- **Substrate-coverage:** `WEEKLY_MATCHUP_RESULT` + `franchise_directory` for tenure-scoping. D44 (SCHEDULE_STRENGTH), D42 (LUCKY_RECORD), and the RIVALRY primordial detector (per Architectural Audit: "Head-to-head history. Tenure-scoping (recent commit `a68a6e0`) adds franchise-tenure filtering") already cover much of this. Tenure-scoping is now a settled pattern per `a68a6e0`.
- **Substrate readiness:** **High.** Tenure-scoped H2H aggregation is already a substrate primitive.
- **Overlap with F1:** **Important.** F1 (Rivalry Chronicle, Contract Card at `Rivalry_Chronicle_v1_Contract_Card.md`) is a *long-horizon rivalry artifact* — multi-season window, head-to-head between two specific franchises, governed narrative restraint. Manager records as a sub-shape of A1 is a *cross-tabulated league-wide view*; F1 is a *single-pair narrative composition*. These are different shapes (view vs. composition) and the overlap is content-pattern, not artifact-shape. If A1 includes manager records as a sub-shape, F1's composition arc is unaffected; A1 displays the table, F1 narrates a specific pair.
- **Overlap with A2 / A3:** Negligible.
- **Product-criteria gradient:** Demonstrates-what-SquadVault-IS — **high**. Sunday/Monday-night-use — **medium-low**. Implementation-risk — **lowest** (tenure-scoping infrastructure done). Learning-value — **medium** (the cross-tabulated table may surface patterns league members don't carry in their heads at the same resolution). §9.2 artisan-frame fit — **medium-high** (Voice Profile §5: "Competitive Hierarchy Is Known and Accepted. KP is the dynasty. Stu has the championship history…"; the H2H table is the substrate of that hierarchy made visible).

#### 4.2.5 The GAF / "Forget it!!!" moments

- **Description:** Culturally significant in-league moments preserved as canonical events. The Voice Profile §5 examples ("Miller went 1-13"; "Pat benched Goedert in the playoffs"; "The Ostrich" — a specific cabin-night legend "Referenced but never fully explained — inside joke"). The shape: substrate moments that have become league lore.
- **Substrate-coverage:** **Heterogeneous.** Three sub-cases:
  - **Substrate-derivable moments.** "Miller went 1-13" is derivable from `WEEKLY_MATCHUP_RESULT` (it's the worst-season-record). "Pat benched Goedert" is derivable from `WEEKLY_PLAYER_SCORE` + roster lineup data, *if* the player-score backfill is complete enough to cover the specific week — see diagnostic D2 below.
  - **Substrate-proxy-derivable moments.** Some lore can be derived as proxies — e.g., "the worst single-week loss for each franchise" is computable as a substrate aggregate even if the league's actual lore tag for that loss (the GAF label) is not stored.
  - **Substrate-non-derivable moments.** "The Ostrich" is per Voice Profile §5 "A specific legendary night at the cabin. Referenced but never fully explained — inside joke." This is **not** a canonical event; it's a cultural artifact that lives in the group chat and in members' heads. The substrate does not now contain it and cannot derive it.

Substrate-non-derivable moments: blocked under §8.2 unless framed as commissioner-curated reference metadata (analogous to `nfl_bye_weeks` and `league_scoring_rules` per the Narrative Angles v2 framing: *"This is NFL calendar data, not league activity. - **Overlap with A2 / A3:** None.

Sunday/Monday-night-use — **low**. Implementation-risk — **mixed** (substrate-derivable: lowest; commissioner-curated: medium, includes the §8.2/§4.4 boundary question). Learning-value — **medium** (the surface that says "here are the moments the league has decided are league moments" is its own kind of surface). §9.2 artisan-frame fit — **highest** for the substrate-derivable shape (this is the Voice Profile §5 pattern at its most direct); **uncertain** for the commissioner-curated shape (it depends on how the annotation surfaces and how it interacts with §4.4 boundary).

## 5. The A1-as-single-surface vs. A1-as-meta-surface question

This is the most consequential product question this memo surfaces.

### 5.1 Reading 1 — A1 as a single surface

Under this reading, A1's first ship is a Phase 11 surface like E1 — one surface, with one (or a small set of) content type(s), that admits later expansion through subsequent specifications or through content-class admission once the Surface Admission Test ships as a framework artifact.

Concretely: A1 ships championship roll + worst-season tracking (or some similar pair) as v1; blowouts hall, manager records, trades hall, draft history are deferred to (a) subsequent A1 expansions, (b) separate cluster-A specifications (A2 for draft; A3 for full championship bracket), or (c) admitted as content classes once the Surface Admission Test exists.

The advantages of Reading 1:

- **Mirrors E1's minimalism.** E1 shipped one channel (group-text-paste-assist), one artifact type (`weekly_recap_v1`), one cadence (weekly). E1's specification consciously did not absorb future content-class admission into its v1; it deferred to the Surface Admission Test framework artifact. Reading 1 applies the same discipline to A1.
- **Lowest specification-session risk.** A small, well-bounded v1 spec is achievable in the specification session's timebox; a meta-surface spec is larger by an unknown margin.
- **Compatible with A2 / A3 admissibility carry-forward** as named in Roadmap §2.2. A2 and A3 remain as their own selection chains, sequenced behind A1.
- **Cleaner surface-vs-test boundary.** Reading 1 says: *what content classes belong on registered surfaces is the Surface Admission Test's job, not A1's spec's job.* This respects the framework-artifact deferral per Reset Memo §10.2.

The cost of Reading 1:

- **Within-cluster fragmentation.** Cluster A's three named candidates (A1, A2, A3) each get their own selection chain — three more four-memo chains, six-to-twelve sessions of doc-work, before all three are registered. Whether this fragmentation is *cost* or *appropriate discipline* depends on whether the three are genuinely separate surfaces or content classes within a meta-surface.
- **Foreclosing the meta-surface reading prematurely.** If A1's v1 is shipped under Reading 1 and the league's reception suggests "this should have been a unified historical archive surface," the unified-archive reading becomes a *revision* to the A1 spec rather than the original architectural choice. Revision is fine (the spec's revision-point exists for this); but pre-deciding without testing the meta-surface reading is a choice.

### 5.2 Reading 2 — A1 as a meta-surface

Under this reading, A1 is specified as a *category* of surface that admits multiple sub-shapes (content classes) governed by a shared specification with per-sub-shape invariants. Under this reading, A2 (Draft History Vault) and A3 (Championship Timeline) might be reclassified as A1 content classes rather than as separate surfaces.

Concretely: A1's per-surface constitutional memo specifies the meta-surface's governance (channel, archive, approval, revision-point, content-admission rules) and its v1 ships a starter set of content classes (championship roll, worst-season tracking, maybe blowouts hall). Subsequent content classes are admitted via a content-admission mechanism that *is the Surface Admission Test* — or, alternatively, via a lighter mechanism the meta-surface specifies for itself.

The advantages of Reading 2:

- **Architectural coherence with the "browseable historical archive" framing** in parent §4 Cluster A: *"Browseable league history is exactly the kind of surface a longtime fantasy football league would actually enjoy."* The framing suggests a unified archive, not three separate archives.
- **§8.4 doctrinal-fit certification likely simpler** under one meta-surface than under three separate surfaces (one set of certifications, applied to a content-admission rule, rather than three separate certification sets).
- **Substrate-coverage stories simpler to tell** as a unified historical archive than as three separately-themed surfaces (because the substrate is *one* memory layer; the three surfaces are downstream presentation cuts).

The cost of Reading 2:

- **Forces the Surface Admission Test to ship as a predecessor**, or forces A1 to specify a lighter content-admission mechanism that the Surface Admission Test will later supersede. Both options are real; neither is cost-free. Surface Admission Test sequencing per Roadmap §5 is not predecessor-state-met (the Roadmap §5.1 names predecessor as "first content-class admission attempted" — exactly the moment a meta-surface ships).
- **Specification-session scope is larger** than a single-surface v1.
- **Re-classifies A2 and A3 from "admissible Cluster A surfaces sequenced behind A1" to "A1 content classes."** This is not a doctrinal violation (parent §4 named them as within-cluster sub-question, not as foreclosed separate surfaces), but it shifts the Roadmap §2.2 admissible-set framing.
- **Could push F1's positioning.** F1 (Rivalry Chronicle) is admissible-contingent-on-substrate-readiness per Roadmap §2.3. F1 is a *composition* (narrative artifact), not a *presentation* (cross-tab view); but if A1 is a meta-surface that admits "the rivalry view" as a content class, the boundary between meta-surface presentations and contracted composition artifacts is one more thing the spec session must adjudicate.

### 5.3 The structural analogy to E1's §9.2 layering question

Reading 1 vs. Reading 2 is **structurally analogous to E1's §9.2 layering-question election** as recorded in E1 spec §1:

- E1's Reading 1: Phase 11 first-surface ⊇ Operational Phase B's deliverable, formalized.
- E1's Reading 2: Phase 11 first-surface = product-vision-tier choice, independent of Operational Phase B.

E1 elected Reading 1 at the specification session under §9.2 weighted judgment. The election was the founder's product judgment; this memo's predecessor records the election but does not collapse the question into the disposition.

The analogy: **A1's Reading 1 vs. Reading 2 is a founder product judgment under §9.2.** It is not a doctrinal screen; both readings pass §4.4 / §8.2 / §8.4 / §9.2 / §10.2. The decision is *which reading the founder elects for A1's specification session*. The decision-readiness session or specification session adjudicates; this memo does not.

Per Anti-Drift §10 #3: do not collapse the surface-vs-meta-surface question. This memo registers it for downstream adjudication.

**Confidence on the surface-vs-meta-surface question:** **High** that both readings are doctrinally admissible; **medium** that Reading 1 is the cleaner default under the precedent set by E1's minimalism; **medium-low** that Reading 1 is the *correct* election (this is product judgment, not a doctrinal screen, and the founder's read may legitimately differ).

### 6.2 D2 — historical-data-completeness probe

**What:** A probe of whether the canonical-event ledger is complete back to PFL Buddies' inception year for the event types A1's sub-shapes consume. Specifically:

- `WEEKLY_PLAYER_SCORE` — the player-level scoring substrate. The decision-readiness session should run `SELECT season, COUNT(*) FROM memory_events WHERE event_type='WEEKLY_PLAYER_SCORE' GROUP BY season` against `.local_squadvault.sqlite` and confirm coverage across all 17 seasons. **Medium confidence on coverage; probe required.**

If WEEKLY_PLAYER_SCORE coverage stops in 2018 (hypothetical), then "the most productive auction investment in league history" claims for 2008-2017 are unavailable, and the sub-shape's pitch as "17 seasons of digital history" is overstated by a decade.

**What to run:** Simple SQL probes against the canonical event ledger, season-by-season, per event type. Document the coverage matrix. Where gaps exist, characterize them (full-season gap; partial-season gap; specific-event-type gap).

**What gates decision-readiness:** A coverage matrix that the decision-readiness session can read to confirm or qualify A1's sub-shape v1 scope. Where coverage is partial, the disposition options are: (a) limit the sub-shape's scope to the covered window (e.g., "draft history hall back to 2018"); (b) note the gap explicitly in A1's display (per §2.3 silence-over-speculation: "the league's auction history is digitized back to YYYY"); (c) defer the sub-shape until backfill ships.

**Confidence this diagnostic is necessary:** **High** — completeness gaps would materially affect what A1's v1 can claim, and uncharacterized gaps risk speculation per §2.3.

### 6.3 D3 — GAF / Forget it!!! canonicalization framing

**What:** A framing-decision diagnostic, not a query-style probe. What does A1 do with the culturally-significant moments that live in league memory but not in the canonical substrate?

**Three candidate dispositions:**

- **D3-Alpha — Substrate-derivable proxies only.** A1 ships only the lore moments derivable from canonical events (e.g., "Miller went 1-13" surfaces as the worst-season-record presentation; "Pat benched Goedert in the playoffs" surfaces — if WEEKLY_PLAYER_SCORE backfill covers it — as a specific lineup-decision data point). The GAF *label* (the league's affectionate name for the moment) is *not* stored or surfaced; A1 presents the substrate facts that the league has decided are lore-moments. The Voice Profile §5 guidance ("Never speculate about what owners are thinking, feeling, or planning — these guys would call that out immediately") is respected. This is the §2.3 silence-over-speculation-pure shape.
- **D3-Beta — Commissioner-curated lore annotation layer.** A1 ships substrate-derived moments AND a commissioner-curated annotation table that ties specific moments to specific league-lore labels (e.g., "Miller's 1-13 → 'The Worst Season'", "Pat-Goedert-W12-2023 → 'The Goedert Game'"). The annotation table is reference metadata (analogous to `nfl_bye_weeks` and `league_scoring_rules` per `Narrative_Angles_v2_Definitive_Scope.md` Dimensions 10-11 framing); it is commissioner-write, append-only, and admissible under §8.2 as reference metadata rather than as new canonical events. **Open question:** does this framing collide with Cluster B's failure modes? A commissioner-curated lore annotation is a much narrower shape — a few rows of (season, week, franchise, label) — analogous to the `franchise_directory` per-season name-resolution table. The narrowness may put it on the §8.2 admissible side of the line. **Question for decision-readiness:** confirm or deny.
- **D3-Gamma — Defer the lore-moments dimension entirely.** A1's v1 ships the matchup-derived sub-shapes (championship roll, worst-season tracking, blowouts hall, manager records) and does not address GAF/lore moments. Lore-annotation work, if elected at all, sequences as either (a) a later A1 expansion via the Surface Admission Test framework, or (b) a separate future surface specification — perhaps reopening the Cluster B question with the curated-annotation framing in mind.

**Why this matters:** The GAF moments are the **highest §9.2 artisan-frame fit content** A1 could ship (parent §4 anchors A1's §9.2 fit on Voice Profile §5's *"affectionately remembers mistakes"* line, which is exactly the GAF pattern). But the most §9.2-fitting content is also the most substrate-non-trivial. The framing decision shapes A1's first ship materially.

**What to run:** No queries. A doctrinal-framing analysis session. Read parent §4 Cluster B rationale; read the `nfl_bye_weeks` / `league_scoring_rules` precedent; read the §4.4 social-surface vs. social-network distinction as applied to lore-annotation; arrive at a framing call. Document the call's reasoning so the specification session inherits a clean disposition rather than a re-litigation.

**What gates decision-readiness:** A framing call that the specification session can operationalize. D3-Alpha is the conservative default per §2.3 silence-over-speculation; D3-Beta is admissible-pending-framing-confirmation; D3-Gamma is admissible-as-deferral.

**Confidence this diagnostic is necessary:** **High** — the GAF question is the largest single uncharacterized dimension of A1's candidate set, and resolving it changes what A1's v1 ships.

### 6.4 D4 — operational rhythm and channel question

**What:** A1 is not weekly-cadenced. A weekly recap fires every week and reaches the league through E1's group-text channel. A Hall of Fame & Shame is event-driven (a new championship lands once per season; a new worst-season is recorded once per season; cross-season records update rarely) and browse-driven (league members may revisit at any time). The operational-rhythm question:

- **Channel.** Through what channel does A1 reach league members? Three options:
  - **A1 distributes through E1's group-text channel** at notable moments (championship lands; season ends; a new record falls). One-off distribution events ride E1's infrastructure.
  - **A1 ships as a separately-accessible archive** (a git-backed markdown collection like E1's `_observations/`-resident archive; or a static web page; or a Notion/markdown export). Browse-cadenced; no push distribution.
  - **A1 ships as both** — distribution events that point to the archive.
- **Cadence anchor.** Per Roadmap §4.3 and E1 spec §8, E1's revision-point is anchored on NFL Week 1 ~2026-09-08. A1 could share this anchor (for cross-surface consistency), or anchor on a different point (e.g., end-of-NFL-season ~February 2027, when a new championship has accumulated; or the auction-draft moment ~late August, which is closer to when the league actively engages with historical context for the new season).
- **"One full cycle" semantics.** Reset Memo §8.4 framing per Roadmap §6: Phase 11 closure-eligibility requires "at least one surface used by the league for at least one full cycle." For a weekly-cadenced surface like E1, "one full cycle" is operationally well-defined (NFL Week 1 → end of regular season → playoff → revision-point). For a static-archival surface like A1, "one full cycle" is less obvious. Candidate readings:
  - **Reading A:** One full season has elapsed since A1's first ship, such that the substrate has accumulated a new full year of canonical events that A1 can incorporate at its revision-point. (~12 months minimum.)
  - **Reading B:** One distribution event has fired, with league reception observed (analogous to E1's Track A first-distribution at `2f7d583`). This is much shorter (~one notable moment, possibly within weeks of ship).
  - **Reading C:** Both — one distribution event observed AND one season-rollover incorporated.

**Why this matters:** A1's revision-point is its operational-cycle anchor; the cycle's definition shapes Phase 11 closure-eligibility timing per §8.4 and shapes how A1's spec frames its own evolution.

**What to run:** No queries. A framing analysis. The decision-readiness session names a candidate disposition; the specification session adjudicates.

**What gates decision-readiness:** A leading-hypothesis on the channel question and the cadence anchor. The "one full cycle" semantics question can defer to the specification session (it's a §8.4 closure-readiness framing question, not a sub-shape question).

**Confidence this diagnostic is necessary:** **Medium-high** — the cadence question is real for any non-weekly-cadenced surface, and naming it early prevents the specification session from re-deriving it under time pressure.

## 8. Operational rhythm and revision-point framing

A1 is not weekly-cadenced. The shape of A1's revision-point is materially different from E1's.

### 8.1 The cadence shape

A1 is **event-and-browse-cadenced.** New canonical events accumulate per the existing platform's weekly cycle; A1's *presentation* of those events updates at moments that are larger-grained than the substrate's update rate:

- **Annually** — when a season ends, a new champion is registered, a new worst-season is recorded, new cross-season records may have fallen.
- **At notable in-season moments** — when a single-week record falls (e.g., a new largest-blowout in league history); when a streak crosses a notable threshold.
- **On commissioner-curated occasions** — anniversary moments; off-season reflection.

The browse-cadence is **continuous** — league members may revisit at any time.

### 8.2 Revision-point options

Three candidate anchors for A1's revision-point:

- **Anchor 1 — NFL Week 1 (~2026-09-08), shared with E1.** Pro: cross-surface consistency; one calendar moment for all Phase 11 revisions. Con: NFL Week 1 is a *consumption-layer* moment (the weekly cadence resumes); A1's relevance is anchored on *end-of-season* moments more than season-start moments.
- **Anchor 2 — End-of-NFL-season (~February 2027), when a new championship has accumulated.** Pro: A1's substrate-side cycle is *complete* at end-of-season; the revision can incorporate a full new season's data. Con: ~9-12 months after Phase 11's current chain advances; long timing.
- **Anchor 3 — League-defined moment (e.g., the PFL Buddies cabin draft weekend in late August).** Pro: this is the moment the league re-engages with historical context for the new season; A1's relevance is highest. Con: cabin draft weekend is a *specific PFL Buddies tradition*; using it as A1's revision-point anchor couples A1's spec to one league's calendar in a way that may not generalize. (Per the Compact's "niche generality" framing: PFL is the v1, but the platform is niche-agnostic-in-principle. League-specific cadence anchors should be parameterizable, not hardcoded.)

**Leading hypothesis on the anchor:** **Anchor 1 (NFL Week 1) for cross-surface consistency, with the specification session noting that A1's revision-point's *substance* — what gets evaluated — is end-of-season-accumulation, not session-start.** This is a "shared calendar, different substance" pattern: A1's revision-point fires at the same moment E1's does, but the questions it asks at that moment are different (E1: did the channel work over the season? A1: did the league actually browse the historical archive over the inter-season period, and does the new-season substrate accumulation need to land as a content-update?).

Confidence on the anchor: **Medium** — Anchor 1 is the cleanest cross-surface default; Anchors 2 and 3 are defensible alternatives that the decision-readiness or specification session may elect.

### 8.3 "One full cycle" semantics for §8.4 closure-eligibility

Per §6.4's D4 framing: the §8.4 floor ("Phase 11 Closure Memo can be authored after at least one Phase 11 surface used by the league for at least one full cycle") needs an A1-shaped reading.

**Leading hypothesis:** **Reading C from §6.4** — one full cycle for A1 = one distribution event fired AND one season-rollover incorporated. The combination captures both *the channel works* (Reading B's content) and *the substrate-accumulation cycle works* (Reading A's content).

Operationally, this means A1's first cycle is plausibly ~12 months from ship (substrate-accumulation), modulated by any earlier distribution event that lands.

This is **slower than E1's first-cycle expectation** (E1's first cycle accumulates over the 2026 NFL season, ~5 months). A1's per-§8.4 closure-eligibility-contributing cycle is longer. The Roadmap §6.3 "plausibly 2026-late or 2027" Phase 11 Closure framing accommodates this; A1's shipping does not accelerate closure-eligibility, but it does add a second Phase 11 surface to the closure-evidence base.

Confidence on the "one full cycle" reading: **Medium** — the framing is anchored on §8.4 substance plus E1's revision-point precedent, but A1's static-archival shape has no direct precedent in the Phase 11 surface set, and the founder's judgment at specification may legitimately differ.
