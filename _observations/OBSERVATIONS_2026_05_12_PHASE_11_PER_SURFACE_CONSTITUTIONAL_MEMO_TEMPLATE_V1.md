## 3. The twelve-section template structure

For each section: **name and one-line purpose**; **classification** (required / mode-flexible / conditional) with reasoning; **abstraction layer** (what every instance must address); **authoring guidance** (what predecessor reads ground the section; what doctrinal anchors apply; what shape the section's output takes).

### 3.1 §1 — §9.2 election

**Purpose.** Record the founder's §9.2 product judgment on the opening framing question the predecessor chain has surfaced, before the spec authoring proceeds against the elected reading.

**Classification:** **Required.** Both E1 and A1 had an explicit Reading 1 / Reading 2 election at §1. E1's was the Phase 11 vs Phase B layering question. A1's was the surface-vs-meta-surface question. The specific question is surface-specific (it depends on what the parent / decision-readiness chain surfaced); the election shape is template-stable.

**Abstraction.** Every spec opens by stating the §9.2 framing question its predecessor chain surfaced (e.g., the decision-readiness memo's §-named adjudication, or the selection-prep's recorded leading hypothesis), recording the founder's election (typically Reading 1 — the minimalist default — with Reading 2 named as the defensible alternative not foreclosed), and carrying reasoning forward by reference from the upstream chain memo where the reasoning was authored. Confidence on the election and on the elected reading is stated.

**Authoring guidance.** Predecessor read: the immediate upstream memo (decision-readiness or its Step 2 framing analog) is the source of the framing question and the reasoning. The election is a recording event, not a re-derivation. Doctrinal anchor: Reset Memo §9.2 (artisan-frame primary success criterion; founder's product judgment over criteria-counting). Shape of output: a short § stating the question, the election, the carry-forward by reference, and confidence labels.

**Variations the template anticipates.** The question is always present in current Phase 11 practice; both registered instances had one. If a future spec session has no framing question to elect — e.g., a surface where every doctrinal axis has been pre-determined by upstream chain — §1 reduces to "no §9.2 election surfaced by the predecessor chain; the spec proceeds against the inherited dispositions" plus a confidence note. The template treats §1 as required; absence is a documented absence, not an omission.

### 3.2 §2 — §-content verification block

**Purpose.** Verify that the doctrinal §-substance the spec depends on is source-anchored, declare the methodology used (fresh re-read / carry-forward / Roadmap §7.5 source-access), and record any drift findings with their dispositions.

**Classification:** **Required.** Both E1 and A1 had §2 verification blocks. E1 §2 discharged Obligation 1 (fresh Reset Memo §-re-read; carry-forward applied because the source was not directly accessible to the chat-session environment) and Obligation 2 (§4.2-vs-§4.3 patch direction; carried forward as standing item). A1 §2 inherited and applied carry-forward (no fresh §-claim surfaced; Roadmap §7.5 source-access procedure not invoked).

**Abstraction.** Every spec verifies the doctrinal §-substance load-bearing on it. The methodology is declared (fresh source re-read, or carry-forward + cross-anchor, or Roadmap §7.5 source-access procedure invoked for fresh claims). Per-§ substance is recorded with cross-anchors and verdicts (confirm / cannot-confirm-but-substance-consistent / fresh-source-needed). Drift findings are recorded with explicit dispositions (patch in this session, or carry forward as standing item, per the conservative-discipline default).

**Authoring guidance.** Predecessor reads: the predecessor chain's verification blocks (the upstream memo's §2 or its analog) supply the per-§ substance and cross-anchors. Doctrinal anchors: Reset Memo §§2.3, 4.4, 8.2, 8.4, 9.2, 10.2 are the routinely-verified set (per A1 spec §11 carry-forward table); additional sections enter the verification block as the spec's content makes them load-bearing. Shape of output: a per-§ table (substance / cross-anchor / verdict) plus disposition paragraphs for any drift findings.

**Variations the template anticipates.** The Roadmap §7.5 source-access procedure may, in future sessions, retire the carry-forward language for routinely-accessed Reset Memo sections (i.e., if the founder runs `git show bb0f325 --name-only` and pastes Reset Memo content into the session, the substance is source-verified for that session and "carry-forward" becomes unnecessary). The template's §2 abstraction admits this: methodology is declared; substance is what matters.

### 3.3 §3 — Identity and scope

**Purpose.** Declare what the surface IS and what the surface is NOT. Bound the surface's content scope (channels admitted, content classes admitted, temporal scope where applicable). Land any spec-stage gaps the decision-readiness chain surfaced (sub-shape pick, format-shift treatment, framing copy, notable-moment definition, etc.).

**Classification:** **Required.** Both E1 and A1 had a §3.1 "what the surface is" and a §3.2 "what the surface is not" structure. A1 added §3.3 (sub-shapes), §3.5 (notable-moment definition), §3.6 (framing copy), §3.7 (format-shift normalization) — these were the spec-gap landings the Step 2 §8.3 chain handed forward. E1 had its own analog subsections in §3 covering channel and content-class admissibility.

**Abstraction.** Every spec declares identity (a one-or-two-paragraph "the surface is X" statement) and non-identity (a bulleted "the surface is not Y, not Z, not W" — at minimum: not the substrate, not the artisan-frame, not any sibling surface easily confused with this one). Surface-specific subsections land spec-stage gaps the decision-readiness chain registered for this section: sub-shape picks, distribution mechanism specifics, framing copy elections, format-shift treatment, notable-moment definitions, temporal-scope declarations, channel-and-content-class boundaries.

**Authoring guidance.** Predecessor reads: the selection-prep memo's identity carry-forward (typically a §3 in the upstream memo) is the source; the decision-readiness memo's §8.3 (or analog) gap list names the spec-stage elections that land in §3 subsections. Doctrinal anchor: Reset Memo §§4.4 (social-surface-vs-social-network) and §8.2 (No-New-Foundations) constrain what the surface can be; the artisan-frame distinction (per E1 §10.2 and Operational Plan §10) constrains what the surface is not. Shape of output: §3.1 identity, §3.2 non-identity, plus numbered subsections (§3.3, §3.4, ...) for surface-specific elections.

**Variations the template anticipates.** The number of surface-specific subsections beyond §3.1 / §3.2 varies materially with the surface's elections-pending state. E1 had relatively few (the surface was already operational at HEAD; its identity and scope had been operationally pre-determined). A1 had five additional subsections because Step 2 had handed forward seven spec-stage gaps. The template does not prescribe a fixed subsection count; subsections are added as the predecessor chain surfaces them. The "what the surface is not" subsection always includes at minimum (a) not the substrate, (b) not the artisan-frame, (c) any sibling surface or framework artifact the predecessor chain registered as distinct.

### 3.4 §4 — Doctrinal compliance (§-by-§ trace)

**Purpose.** Verify the surface's posture against each load-bearing doctrinal section, demonstrating that the surface honors the doctrine in operational specifics.

**Classification:** **Required.** Both E1 and A1 traced §4 against Reset Memo §§2.3, 4.4, 8.2, 8.4, 9.2, 10.2 (per A1 §11 carry-forward table). The trace structure is template-stable; the per-§ verification content is surface-specific.

**Abstraction.** Every spec traces, per load-bearing doctrinal §, how the surface honors that §. The standard six (per the two grounded instances) are: §2.3 five core principles (facts immutable and append-only; narratives derived not fact-creating; AI assists, humans approve; silence preferred over speculation; no analytics, optimization, engagement loops, or prediction); §4.4 Tone Engine boundary and social-surface-vs-social-network distinction; §8.2 No-New-Foundations and provisional-then-promoted filing model; §8.4 six Phase 11 Closure Memo certifications (provisional posture pending source-verified enumeration at Closure session); §9.2 artisan-frame primary success criterion; §10.2 surface-choice is the specification session's call (discharged by this memo's filing).

**Authoring guidance.** Predecessor reads: the parent selection memo's doctrinal screen (per `5a865a1` §4 Cluster A) and the decision-readiness memo's compliance trace are the substrate. Doctrinal anchors: each of the six sections above is the anchor for the corresponding §4 subsection. Shape of output: numbered subsections §4.1 through §4.N, one per load-bearing doctrinal section, each with a "posture" paragraph or table and a confidence label.

**Variations the template anticipates.** The trace set may grow if Roadmap §7.5 source-verified re-reads surface additional load-bearing sections. E1 §4 noted the §4.2-vs-§4.3 drift as a §-citation question that does not affect the trace's substance. The template's §4 admits the trace-set as parameterizable to the spec's content; the standard six are the floor, not the ceiling.

### 3.5 §5 — Operational shape (mode-flexible)

**Purpose.** Register or specify the operational shape of the surface — the channels, the artifacts, the mechanisms, the reception pattern, the lifecycle interaction. The shape is the spec's reference for what implementation produces (Mode B) or what is already at HEAD (Mode A).

**Classification:** **Mode-flexible.** This is the section A1 §11 explicitly named as needing both modes.

**The two modes.**

- **Mode A: Registered-baseline.** The surface has shipped operational code, runbooks, archive structure, or other state at HEAD before the spec was authored. §5 registers the live baseline as the spec's reference shape. Used by E1 (Track A code at `78a1aff`; W7 v27 archive at `2f7d583`; reception capture at `6537ef7` / `e15ddf8`).
- **Mode B: Specified-shape.** The surface has not yet shipped operational state. §5 specifies the operational shape that implementation will produce against the spec, against which the implementation work will be measured. Used by A1 (no shipped code at spec time; implementation arc at `eb6042d` / `97c8bf0` / `642d6dc` came after spec).

**Election criterion.** Has operational state shipped at HEAD before the spec is authored? If yes → Mode A. If no → Mode B. This criterion is mechanical; the spec author runs `git log` against the relevant files / scripts / directories and elects accordingly.

**Abstraction.** Both modes share the same subsection structure (channel, artifact layout, lifecycle interaction, reception pattern, governance interaction); they differ in the verbs used (Mode A: "is" — "the channel IS `group_text_paste_assist`"; Mode B: "will be" — "the channel WILL be archive presentation via git-backed markdown"). The §5 subsection count and topic set is surface-specific within either mode.

**Authoring guidance.** Predecessor reads: for Mode A, the operational shipping memos and the code itself; for Mode B, the decision-readiness chain's operational-shape registrations. Doctrinal anchors: Reset Memo §2.3 (facts immutable and append-only governs operational artifacts at every surface). Shape of output: numbered subsections per operational dimension; mode-appropriate verb tense; mode-stated up front at §5's opening paragraph.

**Variations the template anticipates.** Hybrid modes are admitted: a surface may have *some* operational state at HEAD (e.g., a partial implementation, a scaffold) and *some* unshipped (the bulk). The spec author declares the mode-mixture at §5's opening paragraph and uses the mode-appropriate verb per subsection. The template does not prescribe hybrid handling as a third explicit mode; it admits hybrid as "Mode A for these subsections; Mode B for those" within the author's discretion.

### 3.6 §6 — Surface-specific invariants

**Purpose.** Declare the invariants the surface uniquely commits to — what it forbids itself from doing, what it inherits by reference from substrate invariants, what spec-vs-live-baseline disciplines it commits to.

**Classification:** **Required.** Both E1 and A1 had numbered-subsection invariant blocks: append-only inheritance, forbidden-operations enumeration, channel and content-class admissibility, spec-governs-live-baseline.

**Abstraction.** Every spec declares its invariants explicitly. The shape is numbered subsections (§6.1, §6.2, ...) per invariant, with each invariant naming a binding posture and citing the doctrinal anchor or operational precedent that backs it. Both grounded instances include at minimum: append-only at every surface (Reset Memo §2.3); reception-capture-is-voice-iteration-data-not-engagement-data (Operational Plan §10 commitment #4); no follower graph, no engagement loop, no opt-in network mechanism (Reset Memo §4.4); channel and content-class admissibility (revision-point-gated); specification-governs-live-baseline (the live baseline does not silently revise the specification).

**Authoring guidance.** Predecessor reads: Operational Plan §10 commitments (especially #4 verbatim — *"No engagement analytics, ever. Reception capture is voice-iteration data, not engagement data"*); Platform & Writer's Room Compact framings; the parent / decision-readiness memos' doctrinal screens. Doctrinal anchors: Reset Memo §§2.3, 4.4. Shape of output: §6.1 through §6.N as numbered binding postures with anchors.

**Variations the template anticipates.** The invariant set is a floor, not a ceiling. A surface may add invariants that E1 and A1 do not name (e.g., format-shift normalization disposition as a §6 invariant on a surface where format-shift treatment is binding; archive-layout invariants enforced by tests; etc.). The §6.7 "specification governs live baseline; live baseline does not silently revise specification" provision is universal and should be the last numbered subsection — it is the spec-vs-operational-reality discipline that backs every other invariant.

### 3.7 §7 — Governance

**Purpose.** Record decision-authority for every meaningful decision the surface admits — who decides, with what mechanism, against what gating event.

**Classification:** **Required.** Both E1 and A1 had decision-authority tables at §7.

**Abstraction.** Every spec records its governance in a decision-authority table (decision / authority / mechanism), supplemented by a member-side-rights paragraph clarifying that league members are not "users," are not subjects of analytics, are not audience-segmentation targets. The decision set includes at minimum: artifact approval (DRAFT → APPROVED); distribution authorization (where applicable); content / sub-shape revision; framing-copy revision; format-shift treatment revision (where applicable); spec amendment at revision-point; spec amendment between revision-points (substantive scope change, by separate memo); promotion of spec from `_observations/` to `docs/`; Map registration tier assignment. Authority is typically commissioner-only for operational decisions, founder-only for spec-level decisions; mechanisms cite the operational artifact (script, runbook, memo discipline) that enforces the authority.

**Authoring guidance.** Predecessor reads: E1 spec §7 (the first registered governance table) is the structural reference; surface-specific decisions are added per the surface's operational shape. Doctrinal anchors: Reset Memo §2.3 (AI assists, humans approve) is the universal authority-shape; Operational Plan §10 commitment #4 (reception is voice-iteration-data, not engagement-data) backs the member-side-rights framing. Shape of output: a Markdown table plus a member-side-rights paragraph.

**Variations the template anticipates.** The decision set expands with the surface's content. A surface with push-distribution mechanisms adds a "push authorization" row. A surface with sub-shape elections adds rows per shape. A surface with format-shift treatment adds a format-shift-revision row. The template does not prescribe a fixed decision count; it requires only that every meaningful decision be in the table.

### 3.8 §8 — Revision-point

**Purpose.** Specify the moment(s) at which the spec is re-evaluated against accumulated operational evidence, the anchor's reasoning, the alternatives considered, the triggered-revision provision for anchor-independent revisions, and the promotion criteria for moving the spec from `_observations/` to `docs/`.

**Classification:** **Required.** Both E1 and A1 had §8 revision-point sections. Both elected NFL Week 1 ~2026-09-08 as primary anchor under the "shared calendar, different substance" pattern per Roadmap §4.3 / A1 selection-prep §8.2.

**Abstraction.** Every spec elects a primary revision-point anchor (calendar-fixed moment, event-anchor, or hybrid); states what the revision-point evaluates (channel fit, voice fit, invariant tension, promotion readiness, surface-specific questions); records alternative anchors considered and not-elected with their trade-offs; provides triggered-revision (anchor-independent: any operational finding that surfaces a spec gap may trigger revision between primary anchors); names promotion criteria (typically: at least one full cycle accumulated AND the §8 anchor has fired AND the founder has elected promotion); and — where applicable — defines "one full cycle" semantics per the surface's cadence shape (Reset Memo §8.4 closure-eligibility tie).

**Authoring guidance.** Predecessor reads: E1 spec §8 (the first registered revision-point precedent); the selection-prep / decision-readiness memos' revision-point framing (typically a §8 in the selection-prep). Doctrinal anchors: Reset Memo §6.3 (provisional → promoted filing model); Tier 5 Live Observation Cadence Doctrine at `1cf4142` (provisional-in-`_observations/`, promoted-to-`docs/`-after-empirical-validation precedent). Shape of output: §8.1 primary anchor; §8.2 reasoning; §8.3 "one full cycle" semantics where the surface has a non-weekly cadence; §8.4 promotion criteria; §8.5 alternative anchors (not elected, recorded for completeness); §8.6 triggered revision (anchor-independent).

**Variations the template anticipates.** The anchor specifics are surface-specific: a weekly-cadenced consumption-layer surface (E1) and a browse-cadenced historical-archive surface (A1) both elected NFL Week 1 by judgment but for different reasoning (E1: cross-cycle reception-data accumulation; A1: cross-surface consistency under "shared calendar, different substance"). The "one full cycle" semantics subsection is conditional on the surface having a non-weekly cadence (A1 needed §8.3; E1's "one full cycle" was operationally well-defined by the weekly cadence and did not need a dedicated subsection). The template admits the subsection-set as parameterizable: §8.1, §8.2, §8.4, §8.5, §8.6 are floor; §8.3 is conditional on cadence shape.

**Purpose.** Record what remains admissible after this surface ships, name within-cluster sequencing, document carry-forward of adjacent clusters and substrate-readiness-gated surfaces, and update the Roadmap admissible-surface-set.

**Authoring guidance.** Predecessor reads: the parent surface-selection memo (the cluster-screening source); the Roadmap §2.2 admissible-set registration; the current surface's selection-prep §9 cluster carry-forward (the immediate upstream). Doctrinal anchors: Reset Memo §10.2 (surface choice is the specification session's call — applies to subsequent surface selections, not foreclosed by this surface's selection). Shape of output: §9.1 cluster-of-this-surface within-cluster sequencing; §9.2 / §9.3 / §9.4 adjacent-cluster / F1 / Cluster-B-or-other carry-forwards; §9.5 Roadmap admissible-surface-set update (post-this-surface).

**Variations the template anticipates.** The §9 section's content shrinks as Phase 11 progresses; at Phase 11 closure when the admissible set is empty, §9 either reduces to a one-line "admissible set empty per Phase 11 Closure Memo" or the template revises post-closure. For Phase 11 active surface track (where this template ships), §9 is required. The template flags this as a known evolution point and records the open question in §5 (Template-evolution provision) rather than pre-deciding.

### 3.10 §10 — Prior-attempt findings (conditional)

**Purpose.** Where the spec session was preceded by one or more failed prior attempts with surfaced findings, discharge each finding explicitly with disposition. Where no prior attempts surfaced findings, record absence-of-findings briefly with confidence label.

**Classification:** **Conditional.** Required IF the spec session was preceded by one or more failed prior attempts with surfaced findings. Otherwise minimal/single-paragraph with confidence on absence-of-findings.

**Trigger condition.** The spec session inherits, from its predecessor chain, an explicit list of prior-attempt findings to discharge. The findings are typically surfaced in the parent surface-selection memo's predecessor preamble (where the May 10 prior attempt's three findings entered E1's chain) or in any prior failed memo whose findings carry forward to the spec session. If no such list exists in the predecessor chain, §10 is minimal.

**Abstraction.** Where §10 is required: per finding, name the finding (1-2 sentence summary), state how the spec discharges it (typically by reference to another section of this spec that addresses the finding's substance), and record the disposition (discharged / carries-forward-as-standing-item / closed-by-other-mechanism). Where §10 is minimal: one paragraph stating "no prior-attempt findings; chain proceeded cleanly per the four-memo discipline (selection-prep / decision-readiness / specification / registration)" with a confidence label.

**Authoring guidance.** Predecessor reads: the parent surface-selection memo's predecessor preamble (the canonical findings source); any failed memo whose findings carry forward to the spec session. Doctrinal anchors: Reset Memo §2.3 (silence preferred over speculation — findings carry forward honestly rather than dissolving silently). Shape of output: numbered subsections (§10.1, §10.2, ...) per finding when required; single paragraph when minimal.

**Variations the template anticipates.** The §10-conditional-vs-minimal flip is binary, gated by the predecessor chain. Future spec sessions where neither E1's nor A1's pattern applies (e.g., a third surface that has both prior findings AND a separate "carry-forward findings from a different chain" set) may need a §10 with multi-source structure; this is a candidate template-revision point if surfaced.

### 3.11 §11 — Adaptations from template (purpose shifted post-ratification)

**Purpose.** Document any structural adaptations the spec makes from the current per-surface constitutional-memo template version. Cumulative adaptations across multiple instances are reviewed for template revision per §5 below.

**Classification:** **Required (purpose shifts post-template-ratification).** Both E1 §11 and A1 §11 contained template-candidacy notes (E1 proposing the structure as candidate; A1 documenting adaptations from E1's structure). Post-template-ratification (i.e., after this memo's filing), §11's purpose shifts: it is no longer "proposal of template structure" but "documentation of adaptations from current template version."

**Abstraction.** Every spec authored against template v1 (or any subsequent version) records, in §11, any structural adaptations: section additions, subsection changes, mode-flex variations, conditional-section triggers, or other deviations from the template's prescribed structure. If no adaptations, §11 records "no adaptations from template v1" with confidence on absence-of-adaptations. The adaptations recorded across multiple instances are reviewed by future template-revision sessions per §5 below.

**Authoring guidance.** Predecessor reads: the current template version (this memo for v1; any subsequent version when ratified). Doctrinal anchors: this memo's §5 template-evolution provision. Shape of output: per-adaptation subsection or a single paragraph "no adaptations" with confidence label.

**Variations the template anticipates.** §11 is required at every spec session post-ratification; its content is conditional on whether adaptations exist. The template does NOT recurse — this memo itself does not contain a §11; per scope-item #4 in the session brief, the template's own template-evolution provision (§5 below) serves the analogous purpose. The template-template self-reference loop is bounded at one level.

### 3.12 §12 — Confidence labeling

**Purpose.** Record per-claim epistemic posture: highest-confidence claims with anchors, medium-high claims with anchors and caveats, medium claims with the under-determination named, limitations-to-flag for claims the memo deliberately does not make or that depend on accumulated empirical evidence.

**Classification:** **Required.** Both E1 §12 and A1 §12 had this structure with four subsections (highest / medium-high / medium / limitations).

**Abstraction.** Every spec ends with a confidence-labeling block. The four-subsection structure is the standard. Highest-confidence claims are the ones the spec stakes its substance on; medium-high are well-grounded but admit defensible alternatives; medium are operator-judgment with the under-determination named; limitations-to-flag (or: claims deliberately not made) captures what the spec is silent on by deliberate design (silence-over-speculation per Reset Memo §2.3).

**Authoring guidance.** Predecessor reads: E1 §12 and A1 §12 are the precedent structure. Doctrinal anchors: Reset Memo §2.3 fifth principle (silence preferred over speculation) backs the limitations-to-flag subsection. Shape of output: four numbered subsections (§12.1 highest / §12.2 medium-high / §12.3 medium / §12.4 limitations or deliberate-silences), each with bulleted claims and explicit confidence-anchor pairs.

**Variations the template anticipates.** A spec with thin evidence base may have a heavier §12.3 / §12.4 (more medium-confidence and more limitations) and a thinner §12.1 / §12.2. A spec with thick evidence may have the inverse. The four-subsection structure is template-stable; the weight distribution is content-driven.

## 5. Template-evolution provision

The template is revised when a new spec session surfaces a structural pattern the template did not anticipate. The mechanism, anchored on the precedent pattern from E1 § 11 (which proposed the structure as candidate) and A1 §11 (which documented two adaptations):

### 5.1 Per-instance §11 documentation

Each new spec authored against template v1 (or any subsequent version) documents, in §11, any structural adaptations from the current template version. If no adaptations, §11 records "no adaptations from template v1" with confidence label.

### 5.2 Cumulative adaptation review

When two or more spec sessions independently surface the same adaptation pattern (or sufficiently-related patterns), the adaptation is a candidate for template revision. Single-instance adaptations may be incident-specific and do not necessarily roll into a template revision; multi-instance adaptations indicate a template gap.

### 5.3 Template revision sessions

A template revision session is its own framework-artifact authoring session, following the same provisional-then-promoted filing discipline this memo applies. The revision session:

1. Reads the §11 adaptation records from each spec authored against the current template version.
2. Identifies adaptation patterns warranting revision.
3. Authors template v(N+1) abstracting the patterns; files provisional in `_observations/`; promotes to `docs/` (or wherever the current template lives) after the v(N+1) template has been exercised by at least one new spec session.
4. Records v(N+1) as the current template version going forward; v(N) becomes historical reference.

The revision session does NOT silently edit v(N); v(N) is append-only at its committed SHA. v(N+1) supersedes v(N) by reference, the same way memo amendments supersede prior versions.

### 5.4 Open evolution points already named

The following template provisions are flagged as plausibly under-determined by two instances and may evolve as additional instances accumulate:

- **§9 cluster carry-forward at Phase 11 closure.** When the admissible set is empty (Phase 11 Closure Memo authored), §9's load-bearing weight diminishes. The template currently treats §9 as required during the Phase 11 active surface track and defers the post-closure shape to a future revision session.
- **§10 prior-attempt findings shape under multi-source carry-forward.** Both grounded instances have single-source patterns (E1: findings from one prior attempt; A1: no findings). A future spec with multi-source findings (e.g., findings from a failed prior attempt AND carry-forward findings from a different chain) may surface a multi-source §10 shape the template does not currently structure.
- **§5 hybrid-mode handling.** Both grounded instances were pure Mode A or pure Mode B. A future surface with materially-mixed Mode-A-for-some-subsections and Mode-B-for-others may surface a hybrid-mode explicit structure the template currently admits only at author discretion.
- **§2 source-access maturity.** As Roadmap §7.5 source-access procedure matures and the chat-session-environment-Reset-Memo-access constraint relaxes, §2's carry-forward language may be retired in favor of routine source-verification. The template currently admits both postures.

These are recorded openly per Reset Memo §2.3 (silence preferred over speculation): naming the under-determination is more honest than pre-deciding under thin evidence. Future template revision sessions adjudicate against accumulated instance data.

**Confidence on the evolution provision:** **High** (the precedent pattern is documented in E1 §11 → A1 §11; the revision-session structure is template-stable by analogy to the per-surface revision discipline at §8).
