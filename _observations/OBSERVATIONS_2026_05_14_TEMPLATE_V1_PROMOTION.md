per-surface constitutional-memo template. It recorded two adaptations relevant to the template
candidacy: (a) section 5 mode-flex -- E1 operated in Mode A (live baseline already at HEAD);

section 10 conditionality -- E1's section 10 discharged three prior-attempt findings; a future
surface with no prior-attempt findings should not be required to structure a section 10 for

before ratification. They are absorbed into template v1's section 3.5 (mode-flexible
classification) and section 3.10 (conditional classification). E1 section 11 is counted as the
first grounding instance, not as a post-ratification exercise.

This finding reinforces template v1.0's promotion-eligibility. It does not require a template
revision. It is registered in the Roadmap seasons-count revision memo section 8.1 at `c4b4436`
for the Roadmap's next full revision; this memo cites it and does not re-analyze.

**Adaptation count from A3:** Zero substantive. The anchor-election-agnosticism observation

## 5. Elected Map tier

**Tier 4 -- Operational Control & Build Discipline.**

Reasoning: Tier 4 governs how the work is done, not what it produces. The per-surface
constitutional memo template governs the process of authoring per-surface constitutional
memos -- a build-discipline artifact for the Phase 11 surface specification track. The
existing Tier 4 entries (Engineering Handoff Checklist, Development Playbook, Recap Review
Heuristic) govern how code and recaps are written and reviewed. The template governs how
per-surface constitutional memos are written. Same family.

Tier 3 (Technical Authority) is too high: the template governs document authorship, not
system behavior or inter-module contracts. Tier 5 (Archival & Reference) understates the
template's active-use status -- every future spec author opens the skeleton at session start.

The skeleton companion is registered at the same tier as the template, as a companion
artifact. Map entry registers both as a unit: "Per-Surface Constitutional Memo Template v1.0
(+ skeleton companion)."
