## Purpose

This memo is the cert-5 primary exhibit. Cert-5 asks whether framing drift occurred
during Phase 11 and what was done about it. The Document of Record records the answer
in one sentence; this memo is its standing, citable form so the Closure Memo can point
at a dated `_observations/` entry rather than re-derive it. Per Charter v1.0 section 7:
"catching drift is a success, not an embarrassment." This memo is filed in that spirit.

## What happened (the immutable record)

The 2026-06-09 vision sessions surfaced three engagement-shaped framings. Each was
caught and reframed BEFORE ANY BUILD. No code, schema, or surface was constructed under
any of the three framings; the catch happened in brainstorming, which is the strongest
possible ledger entry (DoR W.7: "'founder caught it in brainstorming' is the strongest
possible ledger entry").

The three are quoted verbatim from the Document of Record below, each paired with the
reframing that replaced it and the surface or standing law the reframing now lives in.

### 1. "drive constant app/revisits"  ->  worthy-of-visits

The engagement framing: build to drive constant return visits. Reframed to the
worthy-of-visits principle - the archive earns visits by being worth visiting, never by
manufacturing pressure to return. This is now standing law for Tracks W and L and is
enforced negatively in DoR Part 8: "No feeds, badges, streak-dopamine, or notification
pressure." (Cross-ref: the same framing appears in the negative register of the Product
Vision and Storytelling Architecture v1.0, `_observations/`, line 246: "Engagement loops
designed to drive return visits" - listed among what SquadVault does not do.)

### 2. "constantly updated feed"  ->  corkboard

The engagement framing: a constantly updated feed. Reframed to the corkboard (Unit W.3):
"Not a feed: no infinite scroll, no algorithmic ordering, no unread counters, no badges."
Finite, dated, chronological, commissioner-published. Push stays per D4.1-Gamma
(archive-resident + push at notable moments), not a stream.

### 3. "engagement evaluated to surface storylines"  ->  words-become-facts, behavior-never-measured

The engagement framing: evaluate member engagement to surface storylines. Reframed to
the Track W / Track L standing law (DoR Part 3): "member words can become facts
(attributed, consented, append-only); member behavior is never measured. No telemetry,
no autonomous publication, no engagement loops." Storylines surface from the verified
substrate and consented testimony - never from measuring how members behave.

## Why this is a success, not a failure

The three framings are the natural gravity of any product built for a community; they
arrive uninvited in any honest brainstorm. The discipline is not in never voicing them -
it is in catching each one before it reaches a build and reframing it against the
charter's standing prohibitions (Charter Authority line: "No analytics, optimization,
engagement loops, or prediction - ever."). All three were caught at the framing layer.
Zero reached architecture. That is the cert-5 result this memo records.
