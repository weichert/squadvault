This memo is the sequencing artifact for the Phase 11 surface track. It registers (a) the admissible set of Phase 11 surfaces with their sequencing dependencies, (b) the framework artifacts deferred per Reset Memo §10.2 with their predecessor conditions, (c) the Phase 11 closure-memo certifications source-verified against Reset Memo §8.4, (d) the standing-items list re-classified after this session's empirical findings, and (e) revision-point provisions.

**This memo is:**

- A scoped horizon-document covering the Phase 11 surface track from current state through Phase 11 closure

- A registration of the framework artifacts (Surface Admission Test, Doctrine-to-Product Translation Table, per-surface constitutional-memo template) with the predecessor-state required for each
- A registration of the six Reset Memo §8.4 closure certifications, source-verified this session

**This memo is not:**

- **A plan to project end.** Per Platform & Writer's Room Compact framing, the project horizon is open ("long-term lens, not near-term plan"). The roadmap covers Phase 11 only; Phase 12+ is contingent on the Closure Memo's certifications per Reset Memo §8.4, which the roadmap does not pre-determine.
- **An authoring of any specific surface or framework artifact.** Each specification, including each per-surface constitutional memo, is its own session. The roadmap sequences; it does not author.
- **A revision of doctrine.** §2.3, §4.2 / §4.3, §4.4, §6.2, §6.3, §6.4, §6.5, §8.2, §8.4, §9.2, §10.2 bind. Applied here, not contested.

The roadmap reduces the option space; it does not collapse it to one.
- **A binding contract on operational Phase B work.** Operational Plan §8 Phase B proceeds on its own cadence (Tracks B + D — reception capture, scheduler wiring, weekly digest, six-cycle rhythm). The roadmap consumes Phase B's output (reception data) at revision-points; it does not direct Phase B's pace or scope.

**Why this memo exists, recorded honestly:** the session-by-session discipline of the prior surface selection arc (parent → decision-readiness → specification) is correct and load-bearing; this roadmap does not replace it. What the roadmap adds is a registered list of what's *known to be on the horizon* — admissible surfaces, deferred framework artifacts, the closure target — so that no surface selection session has to re-derive the option set from doctrine each time. The roadmap is a working-memory aid for the Phase 11 surface track, not a substitute for the selection discipline.

The roadmap consumes Phase B's output (reception data) at revision-points; it does not direct Phase B's pace or scope. New admissible surfaces may emerge (Compact-enumerated content forms not in the current inventory); standing items may discharge; framework artifact predecessor-state may shift; the Closure Memo eligibility may activate. ### 8.6 The roadmap's revision discipline matches the surface-spec discipline

Roadmap revisions are append-only at the artifact layer — new dated memos that amend or supersede this one, not silent edits. The same Tier 5 doctrine that governs per-surface constitutional memos governs this roadmap: provisional in `_observations/`; promotion to `docs/` and Map registration after empirical validation; revision-point-driven re-evaluation.

### 8.7 What the roadmap is not authorized to do

- Bypass the four-memo chain for any surface specification.
