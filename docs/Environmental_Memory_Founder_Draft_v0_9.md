## Executive Vision

Environmental Memory is the philosophy that places are first-class memories. Rather than
treating coach offices as customizable game spaces, SquadVault treats them as evolving
personal museums. The office belongs to the coach across their fantasy football lifetime,
while each league contributes a new chapter to that lifetime.

## Core Principles

- Memory before customization.
- Curation before decoration.
- Identity before engagement.
- Permanence before novelty.
- Shared league memories remain with the league.
- Personal spaces remain with the coach.
- AI listens, composes, and proposes — it never invents identity.

## Environmental Memory System (EMS)

EMS is a product capability responsible for expressing identity spatially. It consumes
onboarding preferences, explicit user edits, and earned memory artifacts. EMS never changes
canonical league memory; it only renders environments from approved data.

## Onboarding Philosophy

Onboarding is a conversation rather than a survey. Casual questions about hobbies, beverages,
music, seasons, workspace, sports, collections, travel, decor, and personality establish an
Environmental Profile used to generate a first office reveal that feels immediately familiar.

*(Ratified amendment per Phase 0 memo 4.2: the profile is explicit — reviewed, editable, and
approved by the coach before any office renders from it.)*

## Office Reveal

The office reveal should be cinematic. The user finishes onboarding, enters a warmly lit Tahoe
office, and discovers familiar details without being told why they are there. Recognition —
not explanation — is the emotional goal.

## Architecture

- **Fixed:** architecture, room proportions, lighting language, camera composition.
- **Curated:** props, books, mugs, artwork, decor, collectibles, display cases.
- **Persistent:** office identity across seasons.
- **Portable:** office travels with the coach between SquadVault leagues.

## Memory Model

**League Memory:** recaps, rivalries, championships, constitutions, Hall of Fame.

**Coach Memory:** office, personal decor, hobbies, collections, favorite objects.

**Hybrid:** rings, awards, personal displays of league-earned artifacts.

## Multi-League Vision

A coach has one lifetime office. Every league becomes a doorway from that office. League
artifacts remain canonical within their originating league while personal displays may
celebrate those achievements. Joining a new league enriches the existing office rather than
creating a new identity.

## Office Evolution

Rooms age gracefully. Leather softens, books accumulate, shelves fill, photographs appear, and
memorabilia grows organically over years. The room should feel inhabited rather than updated.

*(Ratified invariant per Phase 0 memo 4.3: the office evolves because history happened — a
season archived, an artifact earned, an event attested — never because a user logged in,
clicked, or spent time.)*

## UI/UX

Provide curated placement slots instead of freeform design. Allow users to swap props, curate
shelves, rotate artwork, and adjust lighting while preserving premium composition.

## Technical Concepts

EnvironmentalProfile, OfficeTemplate, PropLibrary, PlacementSlots, PersonalArtifact,
LeagueArtifactReference, OfficeSnapshot, OfficeVersion, RenderingProfile, MigrationProfile.

## Business Strategy

Environmental Memory encourages long-term subscriptions by increasing emotional value rather
than creating artificial engagement. Multi-league continuity, premium environments,
commemorative books, anniversary collections, and legacy archives provide future monetization
without compromising the platform philosophy.

*(Bounded by the Phase 0 memo 4.3 invariant.)*

## Marketing

Taglines:

- Welcome Home, Coach.
- Every league has a history. Now it has a place.
- Some people remember the championship. We remember where it happened.

Position the clubhouse as the home of a fantasy football lifetime rather than another
dashboard.

## Future Vision

Extend Environmental Memory to the Community Room, Trophy Room, Draft Room, Library, Dock,
Fire Pit, Film Room, and future modules. The long-term destination is a living digital lodge
that quietly chronicles decades of friendships.

## Design Maxims

- A room should feel collected, never assembled.
- Objects should imply stories.
- Users curate, never decorate.
- Silence is part of great design.
- The office belongs to the coach.
- The league belongs to the league.
- Every season should leave a trace.
- The office is never finished — it grows older with its owner.
